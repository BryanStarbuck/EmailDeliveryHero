# Competitive gap analysis — what the others check that we don't (yet)

This is the market map for EmailDeliveryHero. It exists so we can say, with receipts,
that we check **more than our competitors** — and so we always know exactly which
gaps remain to close.

Read it alongside:

* [../README.md](../README.md) — **List of What we Check**: our code-verified list of
  the **23 registered checkers** across six categories.
* [./checks/overview.mdx](./checks/overview.mdx) — the deep per-check specs.
* [./spam_checks.mdx](./spam_checks.mdx) — the master catalog.

The document has three parts:

1. **What we're missing** — the running to-add list: capabilities competitors ship
   that we do not, or only do partially. This is the section we grow.
2. **What we already do that most competitors don't** — our edge, so we don't
   accidentally "add" something we already lead on.
3. **The field, product by product** — every product and the exact things it checks.

> Sourcing note: check rosters below are compiled from each vendor's own
> feature/pricing/tool pages (July 2026). Exact blacklist counts and seed-address
> counts are rarely published in full by any vendor; where a number is quoted it is
> the vendor's own claim.

## Part 2 — What we already do that most competitors don't (our edge)

Do **not** list these as gaps. These are where EmailDeliveryHero already leads the field:

* **DANE / TLSA validation** — across this entire field only **Red Sift** and **URIports**
  meaningfully check DANE. We ship `infra.dane_tlsa` as a core check.
* **DNSSEC chain validation** (flags `bogus`) — essentially **Red Sift-only** among
  competitors. We ship `infra.dnssec`.
* **SMTP server hardening probes** — **open relay**, **VRFY / EXPN** exposure
  (`infra.smtp_security`). MXToolbox does an open-relay + response-time probe; almost
  no one else does VRFY/EXPN.
* **The full transport-security stack in one audit** — STARTTLS + MX cert +
  **MTA-STS + TLS-RPT + DANE + DNSSEC** together. Most DMARC platforms cover MTA-STS/
  TLS-RPT but stop before DANE/DNSSEC.
* **Local-first, self-hosted, free, zero data egress** — the whole audit runs on
  `localhost` against live DNS and mail-server probes; **no domain data leaves the
  machine**. Every paid competitor is a cloud SaaS that ingests your reports.
* **Local report-email ingestion** — we parse DMARC aggregate + TLS-RPT reports on
  your own disk (`emails/`), not in someone's cloud.
* **One unified six-category audit** — SPF, DKIM, DMARC(+ARC), Blacklists, DNS &
  Infrastructure, Spam & Content — instead of a single-purpose tool per checkbox.

The strategic read from the research: the **least-covered checks across the whole
field are DANE, DNSSEC, and SMTP-server hardening — and we already have all three.**
Our biggest genuine gaps are **recipient-side reputation telemetry (Postmaster/SNDS/
Sender Score), seed-list placement, and forensic/named-source DMARC reporting.**
