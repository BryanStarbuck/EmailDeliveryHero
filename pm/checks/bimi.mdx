# BIMI ŌĆö Brand Indicators for Message Identification

**BIMI** (Brand Indicators for Message Identification, currently an IETF Internet-Draft) lets a
domain publish a **brand logo** that supporting receivers (Gmail, Apple Mail, Yahoo, Fastmail)
display next to authenticated mail from that domain. The logo is published as a DNS TXT record at
`default._bimi.<domain>` (or `<selector>._bimi.<domain>`) whose value points at an SVG logo (`l=`)
and, for the providers that require it, a **Verified Mark Certificate (VMC)** or **Common Mark
Certificate (CMC)** (`a=`) issued by a Mark Verifying Authority (MVA, e.g. DigiCert or Entrust).
BIMI is not an anti-spam control in itself ŌĆö its deliverability value is indirect but real: it is
the visible reward for getting authentication right, it raises brand trust and open rates, and its
**hard prerequisite is DMARC at enforcement** (`p=quarantine` or `p=reject`), so a BIMI check
doubles as a nudge toward strong DMARC. The classic real-world failure is a domain that publishes a
BIMI record but never reaches DMARC enforcement, or publishes only an SVG with no VMC ŌĆö in both
cases the logo silently never renders.

This check belongs to the **Spam & Content** dashboard category, `checkId` prefix **`content`**
(BIMI sub-family). Read it alongside the sibling specs and do not duplicate them:

  beside.

## 1. What it checks

**The standard.** BIMI is defined by an active IETF draft (`draft-brand-indicators-for-message-identification`)
plus the companion **AVP (Assertion Record)** and **VMC** drafts. A BIMI record is a DNS TXT record
at `<selector>._bimi.<domain>` (default selector name is `default`) carrying a semicolon-delimited
tag list, minimally `v=BIMI1;` and an `l=` (logo URL) tag, optionally an `a=` (authority/VMC URL)
tag. When a receiver processes an inbound message, it: (1) confirms the message passed **DMARC at an
enforcing policy**, (2) selects the BIMI record using the `BIMI-Selector:` message header if present
(else `default`), (3) fetches the `l=` SVG, and ŌĆö for providers that require it ŌĆö (4) fetches and
validates the `a=` VMC/CMC, checking the embedded logo hash matches the SVG.

**Why receivers care.** BIMI is layered strictly on top of authentication: the logo is a UI reward
that only appears once SPF/DKIM/DMARC are correct and the policy is enforcing, which is exactly why
Gmail/Yahoo made it attractive alongside their 2024 bulk-sender rules. It gives no delivery boost by
itself, but a rendered brand logo materially lifts recognition, open rates, and phishing
resistance.

**Common failure mode.** The dominant failure is **publishing BIMI before DMARC is enforcing** ŌĆö the
record parses fine but every receiver ignores it because DMARC is still `p=none`. The second most
common is **SVG-only (no `a=` VMC)**: Gmail and Apple Mail will not show a logo without a valid VMC,
so the brand sees nothing despite a valid record. Others: an `l=` URL that is not HTTPS or 404s; an
SVG that is not the **SVG Tiny Portable/Secure (SVG Tiny-PS)** profile (has scripts, external refs,
animation, or is non-square); a VMC that is expired or from an unrecognized authority.

**How a failure degrades placement.** BIMI failure does not push mail to spam ŌĆö it removes the
visible trust signal, so recipients see a generic monogram/avatar instead of the brand mark, which
weakens recognition and engagement. Because BIMI presence is a signal some filters weakly reward,
its absence is a soft negative for well-known brands.

## 2. Sub-checks

`content.bimi.*` ŌĆö comprehensive. Each remediation hands the user the exact record or step. The
`present`/`syntax`/`dmarc_prereq`/`selector` rows are **first-round pure DNS**; SVG- and VMC-body
rows are **future** (need an HTTPS fetch plus SVG/certificate validation) and are marked (future).

| Sub-check (`checkId`) | What it verifies | How measured | Typical critical / warning finding | Concrete remediation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `content.bimi_present` | A TXT record exists at `default._bimi.<domain>` (or the configured selector) and starts with `v=BIMI1`. | `resolveTxt('default._bimi.<domain>')`. | **warning:** no BIMI record for a brand that should have one (`info` for domains not opting into BIMI). | Publish a TXT record at `default._bimi.<domain>`: `v=BIMI1; l=https://<domain>/bimi/logo.svg; a=https://<domain>/bimi/vmc.pem`. |
| `content.bimi_syntax` | The record parses: `v=BIMI1` first, known tags (`v`, `l`, `a`), valid `;`-separated `tag=value` pairs, no stray tokens. | Tokenize and validate the tag list against the BIMI draft grammar. | **critical:** malformed record ŌåÆ receivers ignore BIMI. | Fix the offending tag; `v=BIMI1` must be first and `l=` must be a valid URL. |
| `content.bimi_dmarc_prereq` | DMARC is published **and enforcing** (`p=quarantine` or `p=reject`); some receivers also require `sp` enforcing and `pct=100`. | Read the domain's DMARC finding (`dmarc.policy`) from the same audit run. | **critical:** BIMI record present but DMARC is `p=none`/absent ŌåÆ BIMI ignored everywhere. | Move DMARC to `p=quarantine` then `p=reject` (see [DMARC](./dmarc.mdx)); until then BIMI will never render. |
| `content.bimi_selector` | If a selector other than `default` is used, the `<selector>._bimi.<domain>` record exists and matches the value that would appear in the message `BIMI-Selector:` header. | TXT lookup at the configured selector; compare against the sample message's `BIMI-Selector` header if provided. | **warning:** message uses a `BIMI-Selector` with no matching `_bimi` record ŌåÆ no logo. | Publish a `_bimi` record for every selector your mail streams reference in `BIMI-Selector:`, or drop the header and use `default`. |
| `content.bimi_l_present` | The `l=` tag is present and non-empty (an empty `l=` is a valid "declined" record but yields no logo). | Parse the `l=` tag. | **warning:** `l=` empty or missing ŌåÆ no logo will show. | Set `l=` to the HTTPS URL of your SVG Tiny-PS logo. |
| `content.bimi_svg_url` | The `l=` value is a syntactically valid **HTTPS** URL that resolves (DNS + reachable host); plain HTTP is rejected by receivers. | Parse URL scheme/host; first round resolves the host via `node:dns/promises`; a full HTTPS `GET` + `200` is (future). | **critical:** `l=` is `http://` or an unresolvable host; **warning (future):** HTTPS `GET` is not `200`. | Host the SVG at an `https://` URL that returns `200`; use a stable, cache-friendly path. |
| `content.bimi_svg_profile` *(future)* | The fetched SVG is **SVG Tiny-PS**: `baseProfile="tiny-ps"`, square viewBox, no `<script>`, no `<a>`, no external references, no animation, Ōēż ~32 KB. | HTTPS fetch of `l=`, then parse/validate the SVG against the Tiny-PS profile. | **critical:** SVG has scripts/external refs (receivers reject); **warning:** non-square or oversized. | Re-export as SVG Tiny-PS (square `viewBox`, `baseProfile="tiny-ps"`), strip scripts/links/external refs; validate with the BIMI Group SVG conformance checker. |
| `content.bimi_svg_square` *(future)* | The SVG `viewBox` is square (1:1); non-square logos are rejected/cropped. | Parse `viewBox` width vs height from the fetched SVG. | **warning:** aspect ratio ŌēĀ 1:1. | Re-crop the artwork to a square canvas and re-export. |
| `content.bimi_vmc` | The `a=` tag (VMC/CMC URL) is present ŌĆö Gmail and Apple Mail will **not** display a logo without a verified mark certificate. | Parse the `a=` tag. | **warning:** BIMI record has `l=` but no `a=` ŌåÆ logo won't render in Gmail/Apple. | Obtain a **VMC** (registered trademark) or **CMC** (non-trademark mark) from a Mark Verifying Authority and publish its PEM URL in `a=`. |
| `content.bimi_vmc_url` | The `a=` value is a valid **HTTPS** URL that resolves. | Parse scheme/host; first round resolves host; HTTPS `GET` is (future). | **critical:** `a=` is `http://` or unresolvable. | Host the VMC PEM at an `https://` URL returning `200`. |
| `content.bimi_vmc_valid` *(future)* | The VMC/CMC certificate is a valid PEM chain, from a **recognized MVA**, not expired, and the mark type matches (VMC = trademarked, CMC = government-issued/other). | HTTPS fetch of `a=`, parse the X.509 chain, verify issuer against the MVA allow-list, check `notBefore`/`notAfter`. | **critical:** VMC expired or from an unrecognized issuer ŌåÆ logo suppressed. | Renew the VMC (they expire ~annually) or reissue from a recognized MVA; update the `a=` URL. |
| `content.bimi_vmc_logo_match` *(future)* | The logo embedded in the VMC matches the SVG served at `l=` (hash/SHA-256 comparison). | Extract the logotype from the VMC extension; compare against the fetched SVG. | **critical:** VMC logo ŌēĀ served SVG ŌåÆ receivers reject. | Re-issue the VMC against the exact SVG you serve, or serve the exact SVG the VMC certifies. |
| `content.bimi_single` | Exactly one TXT record at the `_bimi` name (multiple is ambiguous, like multiple SPF/DMARC). | Count TXT records at the selector name. | **warning:** two or more `_bimi` TXT records. | Delete the extra TXT so exactly one `v=BIMI1` record remains. |
| `content.bimi_dns_health` | The `_bimi` name resolves cleanly (no NXDOMAIN chain, no dangling CNAME to a dead host). | Resolve the name and any CNAME target. | **warning:** `_bimi` is a CNAME to an unclaimed target. | Point `_bimi` directly at the TXT record or a claimed host; remove dangling CNAMEs. |

## 3. How it is measured

Implemented in code:
`~/BGit/Bryan_git/EmailDeliveryHero/code/packages/backend/src/modules/audit/checks/bimi/bimi.check.ts` ŌĆö the `bimiCheck` checker, method `run()`

**First round (pure `node:dns/promises`).** The checker runs
`resolveTxt('default._bimi.<domain>')` (and, for each entry in `CheckContext.selectors` that is
intended as a BIMI selector, `resolveTxt('<selector>._bimi.<domain>')`). It joins each TXT record's
character-string chunks, tokenizes on `;`, and builds a tag map. It validates: `v=BIMI1` is present
and first (`content.bimi_syntax`), exactly one record exists (`content.bimi_single`), `l=`/`a=` are
present (`bimi_l_present`/`bimi_vmc`), and each URL is a syntactically valid **HTTPS** URL whose host
resolves (`bimi_svg_url`/`bimi_vmc_url` ŌĆö scheme + DNS resolution only, no body fetch). The
**DMARC prerequisite** (`content.bimi_dmarc_prereq`) is evaluated by reading the sibling
`dmarc.policy` finding produced in the *same* `AuditResult` rather than re-querying ŌĆö the runner
guarantees the DMARC checker runs before BIMI so BIMI can consume its result; if DMARC is absent or
`p=none`, BIMI's presence is downgraded to `critical` regardless of an otherwise-perfect record,
because it will render nowhere.

**Selector handling.** If the operator supplies a sample message with a `BIMI-Selector:` header, the
checker parses that header's `s=` value and verifies a matching `_bimi` record exists
(`content.bimi_selector`); with no sample message it audits only `default`.

**Future (HTTPS + SVG + certificate).** The body-level sub-checks need a network probe beyond DNS:
an HTTPS client `GET`s the `l=` SVG and validates it against the **SVG Tiny-PS** profile (XML parse:
`baseProfile="tiny-ps"`, square `viewBox`, reject `<script>`/`<a>`/external `href`/`xlink:href`,
reject animation elements, enforce a size cap). A second `GET` fetches the `a=` VMC PEM; the chain is
parsed (via `openssl`/a Node X.509 lib), the issuer checked against a configurable **MVA allow-list**
(`bimi_mva` reference table), validity dates checked, and the embedded logotype hashed and compared
to the served SVG (`bimi_vmc_logo_match`). These are gated behind the same HTTPS/TLS-probe capability
used by `infra.mta_sts`.

**Edge cases & caching.** BIMI TXT records are cacheable at their zone TTL; the checker respects the
scheduler cadence and does not over-query. A **declined** record (`v=BIMI1; l=; a=;` with empty
values) is valid and means "intentionally no logo" ŌĆö treated as `info`, not a failure. Wildcard or
CNAMEd `_bimi` names are followed. HTTPS fetches (future) apply a short timeout, follow one redirect,
and back off on 5xx.

**Severity mapping.** Missing record for a brand ŌåÆ `warning` (or `info` if BIMI not desired); malformed
record / DMARC-not-enforcing with a record present / non-HTTPS URL / invalid VMC ŌåÆ `critical`;
SVG-only-no-VMC / non-square / near-limit ŌåÆ `warning`; declined or not-opted-in ŌåÆ `info`.

## 5. Information schema (database schema)

Reference the shared core tables owned by [Engineering](../engineering.mdx) ŌĆö do **not** redefine
them: `domains`, `sending_ips`, `dkim_selectors`, `audit_runs`, and `findings` (each BIMI
`content.bimi_*` result also emits a normal `findings` row with `category='content'`). BIMI adds one
structured **result** table plus one **reference** table.

```sql
-- Structured result of one BIMI evaluation for one domain in one audit run.
CREATE TABLE bimi_check_results (
    id               bigserial   PRIMARY KEY,
    audit_run_id     bigint      NOT NULL REFERENCES audit_runs(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
    domain_id        bigint      NOT NULL REFERENCES domains(id)    ON DELETE CASCADE,
    selector         text        NOT NULL DEFAULT 'default', -- which _bimi selector was audited
    present          boolean     NOT NULL,                   -- v=BIMI1 TXT record exists
    raw_record       text,                                   -- exact TXT string observed
    svg_url          text,                                   -- l= value
    vmc_url          text,                                   -- a= value (NULL if no VMC)
    dmarc_enforcing  boolean     NOT NULL,                   -- DMARC p=quarantine|reject at audit time
    svg_valid        boolean,                                -- NULL until HTTPS/SVG round runs
    vmc_valid        boolean,                                -- NULL until cert round runs
    vmc_not_after    timestamptz,                            -- VMC expiry (NULL until parsed)
    vmc_issuer       text,                                   -- MVA that issued the VMC (NULL until parsed)
    checked_at       timestamptz NOT NULL DEFAULT now(),
    CONSTRAINT bimi_results_unique_per_run UNIQUE (audit_run_id, domain_id, selector)
);

CREATE INDEX bimi_results_domain_idx  ON bimi_check_results (domain_id, checked_at DESC);
CREATE INDEX bimi_results_run_idx     ON bimi_check_results (audit_run_id);
CREATE INDEX bimi_results_expiry_idx  ON bimi_check_results (vmc_not_after)
    WHERE vmc_not_after IS NOT NULL; -- drives "VMC expiring soon" alerts

-- Reference table: recognized Mark Verifying Authorities (issuer allow-list for VMC/CMC).
CREATE TABLE bimi_mva (
    id             serial      PRIMARY KEY,
    name           text        NOT NULL,          -- e.g. 'DigiCert', 'Entrust'
    issuer_dn_match text       NOT NULL,          -- substring/regex matched against cert issuer DN
    mark_types     text[]      NOT NULL DEFAULT '{vmc}', -- {vmc}, {cmc}, or both
    enabled        boolean     NOT NULL DEFAULT true,
    updated_at     timestamptz NOT NULL DEFAULT now()
);
```

**Mapping to today's JSON file store.** There is no database yet; the store module persists these as
JSON under `~/.email_delivery_hero/`:

* `bimi_check_results` ŌåÆ embedded in each audit file `audits/<domainId>/<auditId>.json` under a
  `bimi` key: `{ selector, present, rawRecord, svgUrl, vmcUrl, dmarcEnforcing, svgValid,
  vmcValid, vmcNotAfter, vmcIssuer, checkedAt }`. The `findings[]` array in the same file holds the
  human-facing `content.bimi_*` rows. One audit file per run is the file-store analog of the
  `(audit_run_id, domain_id, selector)` unique key.
* `bimi_mva` ŌåÆ the `bimi.mvaAllowList` array in `config.yaml` (`{ name, issuerDnMatch, markTypes,
  enabled }`), editable via the admin-only settings.

When the store migrates from files to Postgres it is a single store-module change: the `bimi` JSON
object becomes a `bimi_check_results` row and the config array becomes `bimi_mva` rows.

## 6. When we run the check

**On-demand.** `POST /api/domains/:id/audit` runs the full engine, including the first-round BIMI DNS
sub-checks, immediately ŌĆö this is what the "Run now" button and initial domain add trigger. The
future HTTPS/SVG/VMC sub-checks run in the same call once that capability ships.

**Periodic.** The in-process scheduler (`POST /api/scheduler/run` or the interval job, cadence from
`config.yaml`, default every 6h) re-runs BIMI per domain. The DNS sub-checks are cheap and run every
cycle. When the VMC round exists, the certificate fetch honors a longer effective cadence (daily is
plenty) and the `vmc_not_after` value drives a proactive **"VMC expiring within 30 days"** warning so
the logo never silently drops when the annual cert lapses.

**First-round vs gated.** `content.bimi_present`, `bimi_syntax`, `bimi_single`, `bimi_dmarc_prereq`,
`bimi_selector`, `bimi_l_present`, `bimi_vmc`, and the URL-shape checks (`bimi_svg_url`/`bimi_vmc_url`
at scheme + host-resolution level) ship **first round** ŌĆö pure `node:dns/promises`. Everything that
requires fetching the SVG body or the certificate (`bimi_svg_profile`, `bimi_svg_square`,
`bimi_vmc_valid`, `bimi_vmc_logo_match`, and the `200`-status assertion on the URLs) is **future**,
gated behind the HTTPS/TLS-probe capability shared with `infra.mta_sts`.

**Cost / rate-limit / back-off.** DNS is free and cached at TTL. The future HTTPS fetches hit the
brand's own logo host and the MVA's cert host ŌĆö low volume, one request each per cadence, with a short
timeout, single-redirect follow, and exponential back-off on 5xx. No paid feed or provider API is
required for any BIMI sub-check.

**Regression detection.** The scheduler diffs each run against the prior `bimi_check_results` snapshot:
a BIMI record disappearing, DMARC dropping from enforcing to `p=none` (flipping `bimi_dmarc_prereq`
to `critical`), a `vmc_not_after` crossing into the expiry window, or `svg_valid`/`vmc_valid` going
`true`ŌåÆ`false` are all "new problems" that surface as fresh findings and a category-cell color
regression on the Dashboard.

## 7. First round vs future

| Sub-check | First round (pure `node:dns/promises`) | Future (HTTPS fetch / SVG parse / cert validation) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `content.bimi_present` | Ō£ģ TXT lookup at `default._bimi.<domain>` | ŌĆö |
| `content.bimi_syntax` | Ō£ģ tokenize/validate tag list | ŌĆö |
| `content.bimi_single` | Ō£ģ count TXT records | ŌĆö |
| `content.bimi_dmarc_prereq` | Ō£ģ reads sibling `dmarc.policy` finding | ŌĆö |
| `content.bimi_selector` | Ō£ģ TXT lookup per selector + header compare | ŌĆö |
| `content.bimi_l_present` / `content.bimi_vmc` | Ō£ģ parse `l=` / `a=` tags | ŌĆö |
| `content.bimi_svg_url` / `content.bimi_vmc_url` | Ō£ģ URL scheme (HTTPS) + host DNS resolution | ŌÅ│ HTTPS `GET` must return `200` |
| `content.bimi_dns_health` | Ō£ģ resolve name / dangling-CNAME check | ŌĆö |
| `content.bimi_svg_profile` | ŌĆö | ŌÅ│ fetch SVG, validate SVG Tiny-PS (no scripts/external refs/animation, size cap) |
| `content.bimi_svg_square` | ŌĆö | ŌÅ│ fetch SVG, check square `viewBox` |
| `content.bimi_vmc_valid` | ŌĆö | ŌÅ│ fetch VMC PEM, verify chain + MVA allow-list + expiry |

Rule of thumb: **anything answerable from the `_bimi` TXT record (and the already-computed DMARC
result) ships first**; anything that must fetch the SVG body or the VMC certificate over HTTPS is a
later round. The `Checker`/`Finding` contract is identical in both rounds ŌĆö only the checker's
internals grow ŌĆö so the Dashboard, store, and scheduler never change.

## 8. Acceptance criteria

1. A domain with no `_bimi` record yields `content.bimi_present` = `warning` (or `info` if BIMI is
   not opted into) and no `critical`.
2. A syntactically valid `v=BIMI1; l=https://ŌĆ”; a=https://ŌĆ”` record on a domain whose DMARC is
   `p=none` yields `content.bimi_dmarc_prereq` = **`critical`** with a remediation pointing at
   [DMARC](./dmarc.mdx), because the logo renders nowhere.
3. The same record on a domain with DMARC `p=quarantine`/`p=reject` yields `bimi_dmarc_prereq` = `ok`.
4. A record whose `l=` (or `a=`) is `http://` or an unresolvable host yields the corresponding
   `bimi_svg_url`/`bimi_vmc_url` = `critical`.
5. A record with `l=` but no `a=` yields `content.bimi_vmc` = `warning` ("Gmail/Apple won't show the
   logo without a VMC/CMC").
6. Two `_bimi` TXT records yield `content.bimi_single` = `warning`.
7. A `BIMI-Selector:` header referencing a selector with no matching `_bimi` record yields
   `content.bimi_selector` = `warning`; with a matching record it is `ok`.
8. Every non-`ok` finding carries a concrete `remediation` string containing an exact record/value or
   step, never a generic message; the record-to-publish is copy-to-clipboard in the UI.
9. Each BIMI evaluation writes a `bimi_check_results` row (JSON `bimi` object today) keyed
   `(audit_run_id, domain_id, selector)` with `present`, `raw_record`, `svg_url`, `vmc_url`,
   `dmarc_enforcing`, and `checked_at`; `svg_valid`/`vmc_valid`/`vmc_not_after`/`vmc_issuer` are
   `NULL` until the future round runs.
10. First-round sub-checks use only `node:dns/promises`; `bimi_svg_profile`, `bimi_svg_square`,
    `bimi_vmc_valid`, and `bimi_vmc_logo_match` are absent/skipped in the first round and clearly
    labelled future in the UI.
11. The scheduler flags a BIMI record disappearing, DMARC dropping below enforcement, or a VMC
    crossing its expiry window as a regression (new problem) on the next diffed run.
12. Findings surface under a BIMI subhead in the Spam & Content category and contribute to that one
    dashboard cell's color per the [Dashboard](../dashboard.mdx) roll-up rules.
