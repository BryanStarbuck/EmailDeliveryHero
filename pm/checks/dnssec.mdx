# EmailDeliveryHero — DNSSEC Check

**DNSSEC (DNS Security Extensions, RFC 4033/4034/4035, clarified by RFC 6840)** cryptographically
signs DNS data so a resolver can prove that the answer it received is the exact answer the zone owner
published — no on-path tampering, cache poisoning, or spoofed NXDOMAIN. For email this matters two
ways. First, every other DNS-based control we audit (SPF, DKIM keys, DMARC, MTA-STS TXT, MX records)
is only as trustworthy as the DNS that served it; without DNSSEC an attacker who can forge DNS can
rewrite your SPF `all` qualifier or swap a DKIM public key. Second, **DNSSEC is a hard prerequisite
for DANE/TLSA** — a TLSA record is only meaningful when its zone is signed and validated, because
DANE replaces the public CA trust model with the zone's own signed data. This check answers: *is this
zone signed, does the chain of trust reach the root, and does a validating resolver actually accept
it?*

This check belongs to the **DNS & Infrastructure** dashboard category; all sub-check ids use the
**`infra`** prefix, DNSSEC sub-family (`infra.dnssec_*`). It supersedes the single coarse
`infra.dnssec` row catalogued in [`../spam_checks.mdx`](../spam_checks.mdx) with a full sub-check set.

## 1. What it checks

DNSSEC adds four record types to a zone: **DNSKEY** (the zone's public keys — a Key-Signing Key/KSK
and usually a Zone-Signing Key/ZSK), **RRSIG** (a signature over each RRset), **DS** (a Delegation
Signer digest of the KSK, published in the *parent* zone at the registrar to link the chain of
trust), and **NSEC/NSEC3** (authenticated denial of existence, so a forged "no such name" can be
detected). A resolver that validates walks the chain root → TLD → your zone: it fetches the DS from
the parent, confirms it hashes your DNSKEY, verifies the DNSKEY signs the RRSIG, and only then sets
the **AD (Authenticated Data)** flag in its answer.

**Why receivers care:** a signed, validated zone means the MX, SPF, DKIM, DMARC and MTA-STS records a
receiving MTA reads are provably authentic. It is also the on-ramp to **DANE** (RFC 6698/7671): a
receiving MTA that supports DANE will look up TLSA only for DNSSEC-signed zones and will treat a
downgrade as a hard failure. Google/Yahoo bulk-sender rules don't yet mandate DNSSEC, but it is
increasingly expected for high-trust senders and government/regulated domains.

**Common real-world failure modes:** (a) zone not signed at all — the default for most domains; (b)
zone signed internally but **no DS at the registrar**, so no resolver on Earth can validate it (an
"island of security" that provides zero protection); (c) **expired RRSIGs** — signatures have an
explicit expiration; a broken re-signing job takes the whole domain *dark* (SERVFAIL everywhere) far
harder than any un-signed outage; (d) **deprecated algorithms** (RSASHA1 / algorithm 5 or 7) that
modern validators are phasing out; (e) a botched **key rollover** where the DS no longer matches the
live DNSKEY. Any of these either provides false assurance or, worse (expired RRSIG / DS mismatch),
makes the entire domain unresolvable to validating resolvers — a total mail outage.

**How failure degrades inbox placement:** an un-signed zone is not itself a spam signal, but it leaves
every deliverability record forgeable and blocks DANE. A *broken* DNSSEC chain is catastrophic — mail
to and from the domain stops because validating resolvers (including ~⅓ of the internet and most
large mailbox providers' resolver paths) return SERVFAIL rather than the record.

## 2. Sub-checks

Severity philosophy: an **un-signed** zone is `info` (advisory — you're missing an upgrade, not
broken). A **broken** signed zone (bogus, expired, DS mismatch) is `critical` — it takes the domain
down. Weak-but-working (deprecated algorithm, near-expiry, missing DS on a signed zone) is `warning`.

| Sub-check (checkId) | What it verifies | How measured | Typical critical / warning finding | Concrete remediation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `infra.dnssec_signed` | The zone is signed: at least one **DNSKEY** RRset exists at the apex and RRSIGs cover the zone's records. | `dig DNSKEY <domain>` (via the `dig` helper — `DNSKEY` is not a `node:dns` resolver type); presence of DNSKEY. | **info:** no DNSKEY — zone is unsigned (no tamper-evidence, DANE impossible). | Enable DNSSEC at your DNS provider/registrar (one-click at most: Cloudflare, Route 53, Google Domains, etc.). |
| `infra.dnssec_ds_present` | A **DS** record for this zone is published in the *parent* zone (registrar), establishing the chain of trust. | `dig DS <domain>` at the parent (via the `dig` helper). | **warning:** zone has DNSKEY but no DS at parent — "island of security", validation impossible. | Copy the DS/DNSKEY digest from your DNS provider into your registrar's DNSSEC panel so the parent publishes the DS. |
| `infra.dnssec_validates` | A **validating resolver** accepts the chain: answer carries **AD=1** and RRSIGs verify (not "bogus"). | `dig +dnssec` against a validating resolver (e.g. `1.1.1.1`/`8.8.8.8`) and read the `ad` flag / `AUTHENTICATED DATA`; SERVFAIL with CD=0 but success with CD=1 ⇒ bogus. | **critical:** resolver returns **SERVFAIL / bogus** (broken chain — domain dark to validating resolvers). | Fix the signing chain: re-sign the zone, correct the DS to match the live KSK, or roll back the broken key. |
| `infra.dnssec_algorithm` | Signing algorithm is modern. Prefer **ECDSAP256SHA256 (13)** or **RSASHA256 (8)**; flag deprecated **RSASHA1 (5)** and **RSASHA1-NSEC3-SHA1 (7)**. | Parse the `algorithm` field of each DNSKEY / RRSIG. | **warning:** DNSKEY uses algorithm 5/7 (RSASHA1) — deprecated, being removed from validators. | Roll to a modern algorithm: ECDSAP256SHA256 (13) is compact and fast; RSASHA256 (8) is the RSA fallback. Update the DS after the roll. |
| `infra.dnssec_rrsig_expiry` | RRSIGs are not expired and not near expiry (typical signatures last ~2–4 weeks and auto-refresh). | Parse `Signature Expiration` of the apex RRSIGs; compare to now with a lead-time threshold (e.g. 72h). | **critical:** an RRSIG is already expired (validators return bogus). **warning:** expires within threshold. | Restore automatic re-signing on your signer; if manual, re-sign immediately. Investigate why the re-sign job stalled. |
| `infra.dnssec_nsec3` | Authenticated denial mode is sane: NSEC3 params are reasonable (SHA-1, iterations **≤ 0** per RFC 9276 / low), opt-out flag understood. | `dig +dnssec <nonexistent>.<domain>` → inspect NSEC3PARAM / NSEC3 records; read iterations, salt, opt-out flag. | **warning:** NSEC3 iterations > 0 (excess CPU, some resolvers treat high counts as insecure per RFC 9276); opt-out set on a zone that shouldn't. | Set NSEC3 iterations to **0** and an empty/short salt (RFC 9276); disable opt-out unless you run a large delegation-heavy zone. |
| `infra.dnssec_ds_algo_match` | The **DS digest algorithm** and key tag match a live DNSKEY — the DS actually references the current KSK. | Compute the DS digest (SHA-256, algo 2) of each apex DNSKEY and compare key tag + digest to the published DS. | **critical:** no published DS matches any live DNSKEY (chain broken after a key change). **warning:** DS uses SHA-1 (digest algo 1) — deprecated. | Republish the DS at the registrar so it matches the current KSK; use **SHA-256 (digest type 2)**, never SHA-1 (type 1). |
| `infra.dnssec_key_rollover` *(advisory)* | KSK/ZSK rollover hygiene: reasonable key sizes (RSA ≥ 2048), KSK ≠ ZSK where separated, no stale keys lingering. | Inspect DNSKEY flags (257=KSK, 256=ZSK), key length, count; flag many stale keys or undersized RSA. | **warning:** RSA KSK < 2048 bits; a dozen stale DNSKEYs left in the set (bloats responses, signals abandoned rollover). | Use ≥ 2048-bit RSA or ECDSA P-256; prune retired keys after the rollover completes; follow RFC 6781 rollover timing. |
| `infra.dnssec_chain_complete` | The full chain root → TLD → zone is present and consistent (no missing link between DS at parent and DNSKEY at child). | Walk DS (parent) → DNSKEY (child) → RRSIG; confirm every hop links. | **critical:** DS present but references a key tag absent from the child DNSKEY set. | Re-align the parent DS with the child KSK; the DS key tag must exist in the apex DNSKEY RRset. |
| `infra.dnssec_soa_signed` | Core mail-relevant RRsets (SOA, MX, TXT for SPF/DMARC/MTA-STS) are actually covered by an RRSIG, not just the apex. | For each queried type, confirm an accompanying RRSIG with `+dnssec`. | **warning:** MX/TXT returned without an RRSIG on a "signed" zone (partial signing). | Ensure the signer covers all RRsets, not only the apex; re-sign the full zone. |
| `infra.dnssec_dane_ready` *(advisory)* | Signals to the DANE check whether this zone can host trustworthy TLSA (signed **and** validates). | Boolean AND of `dnssec_signed` + `dnssec_validates`. | **info:** zone unsigned ⇒ DANE not possible (see `./dane_tlsa.mdx`). | Complete DNSSEC first; then publish TLSA records per [`./dane_tlsa.mdx`](./dane_tlsa.mdx). |

## 3. How it is measured

**First round (presence only, via the `dig` helper — not `node:dns/promises`).** Node's stock
resolver **cannot query the DNSSEC record types at all**: `dns.resolve` supports only
A/AAAA/CAA/CNAME/MX/NAPTR/NS/PTR/SOA/SRV/TXT — there is no `DNSKEY` or `DS` resolver type — and even
for the types it does support it returns cached answers without exposing the AD flag or verifying
signatures. So the first round fetches DNSKEY/DS **presence** through the Brew-installed `dig` helper
(`checks/dns-util.ts`), parsing the record fields directly; only `SOA` presence uses
`node:dns/promises` (`resolveSoa`):

* `dig(domain, 'DNSKEY')` → drives `infra.dnssec_signed` (any DNSKEY ⇒ signed).
* `dig(domain, 'DS')` → drives `infra.dnssec_ds_present`.
* `resolveSoa(domain)` presence (via `node:dns/promises`) → `infra.dnssec_soa_signed` (partial; presence heuristic — full per-RRset RRSIG coverage is the deep path below).
* From the DNSKEY answers we read `algorithm` and `flags` (KSK 257 / ZSK 256) and key length ⇒ `infra.dnssec_algorithm`, `infra.dnssec_key_rollover` (advisory, static parse — no validation needed).

Everything requiring **validation or signature contents** is **FUTURE** and needs shelling to `dig`
(Brew-installed) or a validating-resolver library, because the AD flag and RRSIG expiry/verification
are not available through Node's default DNS:

* **`infra.dnssec_validates`** — shell `dig +dnssec @1.1.1.1 <domain> SOA` and parse the header `flags:` for `ad`. Cross-check with a **CD (Checking Disabled)** probe: if `@1.1.1.1 <domain>` is SERVFAIL but `@1.1.1.1 +cd <domain>` succeeds, the data exists but is **bogus** (broken signatures) → `critical`. If both fail it's an ordinary NXDOMAIN/outage, not a DNSSEC verdict.
* **`infra.dnssec_rrsig_expiry`** — `dig +dnssec <domain> DNSKEY` and parse each RRSIG's `Signature Expiration` (format `YYYYMMDDHHMMSS`), compare to now.
* **`infra.dnssec_nsec3`** — `dig +dnssec <random-nonexistent-label>.<domain>` and read NSEC3PARAM (hash, flags/opt-out, iterations, salt).
* **`infra.dnssec_ds_algo_match` / `infra.dnssec_chain_complete`** — recompute the DS digest (SHA-256) locally from the DNSKEY (RFC 4034 §5.1.4: `digest = SHA256(owner_name | DNSKEY_RDATA)`) and compare key tag + digest to the published DS; or use `dig +dnssec +multi` and a validator library (e.g. `dns-packet` + a DNSSEC verifier) to walk the chain.

**Edge cases & operational concerns:**
* **Resolver caching hides the truth.** Node reads a cached upstream answer; to see the authoritative,
  un-cached state we must query the domain's authoritative NS directly (`dig +dnssec @<authNS>`) or a
  known validating resolver. Prefer explicit resolvers over the host default.
* **CD-flag disambiguation** (above) is essential — never report "bogus" from a bare SERVFAIL.
* **Time skew** breaks RRSIG-expiry math: use a trusted clock; a wrong local clock can false-flag
  expiry.
* **Multiple keys** during a rollover are normal — `ds_algo_match` must match *any* live KSK, and
  `key_rollover` must tolerate a transient extra key without flagging.
* **Rate limits / cost:** DNS is cheap; validation via public resolvers (1.1.1.1 / 8.8.8.8) should be
  gently rate-limited and cached per audit run. No paid feed or API is required.

**Severity mapping summary:** unsigned → `info`; signed-but-no-DS or deprecated algo or near-expiry or
SHA-1 DS → `warning`; SERVFAIL/bogus, expired RRSIG, or DS-does-not-match-live-key → `critical`; a
fully signed, validating, modern-algorithm zone → `ok`.

## 4. User interface

DNSSEC contributes to the **DNS & Infrastructure** dashboard cell (column 5). Per the roll-up rule in
[`../dashboard.mdx`](../dashboard.mdx), the cell's color is the worst severity across *all* `infra.*`
findings for that domain; DNSSEC `info` (unsigned) alone does **not** turn the cell amber, but a
broken-chain `critical` turns it dark red. Because a broken DNSSEC chain is a domain-wide outage, when
`infra.dnssec_validates` is `critical` the audit-detail view pins it to the top of the DNS &
Infrastructure group with a "Domain may be unresolvable" banner.

**Audit-detail rendering (React 19 + shadcn/ui).** Findings render as grouped rows under a **"DNS &
Infrastructure › DNSSEC"** collapsible section (shadcn `Accordion`), each row:

* a severity **`Badge`** (green `ok` / blue `info` / amber `warning` / red `critical`) + lucide icon
  (`ShieldCheck`, `ShieldAlert`, `ShieldOff`);
* the **observed detail** (e.g. `DNSKEY: alg 13 (ECDSAP256SHA256), KSK 257 + ZSK 256; DS at parent: yes (SHA-256); AD flag: 1`);
* the **remediation** string with a **copy-to-clipboard** button (shadcn `Button` + `Clipboard`
  icon) that copies the exact registrar step or the DS digest to publish;
* for `dnssec_validates` bogus, a "Why is my domain down?" link into
  [`../spam_checks.mdx`](../spam_checks.mdx).

**Per-domain config inputs (Domains detail page).** DNSSEC needs no per-domain input beyond the domain
itself, but an **admin-only Settings** panel exposes: the **validating resolver(s)** to query
(default `1.1.1.1`, `8.8.8.8`), the **RRSIG near-expiry lead time** (default 72h), and a toggle for
**"validate via `dig` (future)"** vs presence-only first round.

**States.** *Never-run:* the DNSSEC rows show "Never checked" (gray). *Loading:* shadcn `Skeleton`
rows while the query is in flight. *Unsigned (info):* a single blue row — "Zone is not DNSSEC-signed
— optional, but required for DANE" with a link to [`./dane_tlsa.mdx`](./dane_tlsa.mdx). *Future-gated
sub-checks* (validation) render with a small "Deep check" tag until the `dig` path ships.

## 5. Information schema (database schema)

DNSSEC references the **shared core tables** owned by engineering — do **not** redefine these:
`domains`, `sending_ips`, `dkim_selectors`, `audit_runs`, and `findings`. Each DNSSEC sub-check emits
a row into the generic **`findings`** table (`category='infra'`, `check_id='infra.dnssec_*'`). The
table below adds the **structured, parsed** DNSSEC observation for one domain in one audit run.

```sql
-- Check-specific structured result. One row per (audit_run, domain).
CREATE TABLE dnssec_check_results (
    id                    BIGSERIAL   PRIMARY KEY,
    audit_run_id          BIGINT      NOT NULL REFERENCES audit_runs(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
    domain_id             BIGINT      NOT NULL REFERENCES domains(id)    ON DELETE CASCADE,
    signed                BOOLEAN     NOT NULL,             -- DNSKEY present at apex
    ds_present            BOOLEAN     NOT NULL,             -- DS at parent/registrar
    validates             BOOLEAN     NULL,                 -- AD=1 (NULL until validating resolver ships)
    bogus                 BOOLEAN     NOT NULL DEFAULT FALSE, -- SERVFAIL w/ CD=1 success ⇒ broken chain
    dnskey_algos          JSONB       NOT NULL DEFAULT '[]', -- [{keyTag,flags,alg,algName,bits}]
    ds_digest_type        SMALLINT    NULL,                 -- 1=SHA1, 2=SHA256
    ds_algo_match         BOOLEAN     NULL,                 -- published DS matches a live KSK
    nsec3                 BOOLEAN     NOT NULL DEFAULT FALSE,
    nsec3_iterations      INTEGER     NULL,
    nsec3_optout          BOOLEAN     NULL,
    rrsig_earliest_expiry TIMESTAMPTZ NULL,                 -- soonest apex RRSIG expiration
    resolver_used         TEXT        NULL,                 -- e.g. '1.1.1.1'
    checked_at            TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT now(),
    CONSTRAINT uq_dnssec_result UNIQUE (audit_run_id, domain_id)
);

CREATE INDEX idx_dnssec_domain_time  ON dnssec_check_results (domain_id, checked_at DESC);
CREATE INDEX idx_dnssec_expiry       ON dnssec_check_results (rrsig_earliest_expiry)
    WHERE rrsig_earliest_expiry IS NOT NULL;                -- fast "expiring soon" scans
CREATE INDEX idx_dnssec_broken       ON dnssec_check_results (domain_id)
    WHERE bogus = TRUE OR (signed = TRUE AND ds_present = FALSE);
CREATE INDEX idx_dnssec_algos_gin    ON dnssec_check_results USING GIN (dnskey_algos);
```

Optional small **reference table** for algorithm/digest naming and deprecation policy, so the UI and
severity logic don't hard-code magic numbers:

```sql
CREATE TABLE dnssec_algorithms (           -- seeded config, rarely changes (IANA registry)
    algo_num   SMALLINT PRIMARY KEY,        -- 5,7,8,13,14,15,16 ...
    name       TEXT NOT NULL,               -- 'RSASHA1','ECDSAP256SHA256', ...
    deprecated BOOLEAN NOT NULL DEFAULT FALSE,
    notes      TEXT
);
```

**Mapping to TODAY's JSON file store** (per [`../engineering.mdx`](../engineering.mdx) §7). There is
no database in the first round; the audit run is one file
`~/.email_delivery_hero/audits/<domainId>/<auditId>.json`. Within that file:

* the generic findings live in the run's `findings[]` array (each `{checkId, severity, title, detail,
  remediation}`) exactly as every other check;
* the **`dnssec_check_results`** row maps to a `results.dnssec` object embedded in the same run file,
  e.g.:

```jsonc
// audits/<domainId>/<auditId>.json  →  results.dnssec
{
  "signed": true, "dsPresent": true, "validates": null, "bogus": false,
  "dnskeyAlgos": [ { "keyTag": 20326, "flags": 257, "alg": 13, "algName": "ECDSAP256SHA256", "bits": 256 } ],
  "dsDigestType": 2, "dsAlgoMatch": true,
  "nsec3": true, "nsec3Iterations": 0, "nsec3Optout": false,
  "rrsigEarliestExpiry": "2026-07-14T00:00:00Z",
  "resolverUsed": "1.1.1.1", "checkedAt": "2026-07-01T10:05:00Z"
}
```

* `dnssec_algorithms` maps to a static seed under `config.yaml` (`dnssec.algorithms:`) — the same
  place DNSBL zones live — read-only reference data.

When storage graduates to PostgreSQL the reconciliation is mechanical: `results.dnssec` → one
`dnssec_check_results` row keyed by `(audit_run_id, domain_id)`; the `config.yaml` seed → the
`dnssec_algorithms` table. No shape change to `findings`.

## 6. When we run the check

* **On-demand:** every manual audit (`POST /api/domains/:id/audit`) runs the DNSSEC checker as part of
  the DNS & Infrastructure pass. Presence sub-checks always run; validation sub-checks run when the
  `dig`/validating-resolver path is enabled (future).
* **Periodic:** the in-process scheduler ([`../scheduled_checks.mdx`](../scheduled_checks.mdx)) re-runs
  it on the config cadence (default every 6h). **RRSIG expiry makes DNSSEC a high-value scheduled
  check** — a stalled re-signing job silently marches signatures toward expiry, and the periodic run
  catches `infra.dnssec_rrsig_expiry` at `warning` (within lead time) *before* it becomes a `critical`
  domain-wide outage. Consider a tighter cadence (e.g. hourly) for zones flagged near-expiry.
* **First-round vs future:** presence sub-checks (`dnssec_signed`, `dnssec_ds_present`,
  `dnssec_algorithm`, `dnssec_key_rollover`) are **first round** via the `dig` helper (DNSKEY/DS
  presence) plus `node:dns/promises` for SOA presence. Validation,
  RRSIG-expiry, NSEC3 params, and full chain verification are **future**, gated behind shelling to
  `dig +dnssec` / a validating-resolver library — Node does not expose the AD flag or verify
  signatures.
* **Cost / rate-limit / back-off:** DNS queries are cheap and free; the only external touch is public
  validating resolvers (1.1.1.1 / 8.8.8.8). Cache one validation per domain per audit run, gently
  rate-limit, and back off on resolver timeouts (report `info`: "validation unavailable" rather than a
  false `critical`).
* **Regression detection:** the scheduler diffs each run against the previous. A newly-appearing or
  worsened DNSSEC finding is a "new problem" surfaced on the Dashboard — most importantly a transition
  to **bogus/SERVFAIL** (outage) or a **DS-mismatch after a key change** (botched rollover), both of
  which warrant immediate operator attention.

## 8. Acceptance criteria

1. The checker implements the `Checker` interface (`id: 'infra.dnssec'`, a display `label`) and
   returns `Finding[]` with `checkId` values in the `infra.dnssec_*` namespace.
2. For an **unsigned** zone it emits exactly one `info` finding (`infra.dnssec_signed`) whose
   remediation names enabling DNSSEC at the DNS provider/registrar — and does **not** turn the DNS &
   Infrastructure cell amber.
3. For a **signed zone with no DS at the parent** it emits `infra.dnssec_ds_present` at `warning` with
   a remediation instructing the operator to publish the DS/DNSKEY digest at the registrar.
4. First-round presence sub-checks fetch DNSKEY/DS via the `dig` helper (the DNSSEC record types are
   not `node:dns` resolver types; SOA presence uses `node:dns/promises`) and are deterministic against
   mocked `dig`/recorded DNS fixtures in CI.
5. Validation-dependent sub-checks (`dnssec_validates`, `dnssec_rrsig_expiry`, `dnssec_nsec3`,
   `dnssec_chain_complete`) are clearly gated as future and **degrade gracefully** to `info`
   ("validation unavailable") when the `dig`/validating-resolver path is disabled — never a false
   `critical`.
6. A **bogus** chain is reported `critical` only after the CD-flag disambiguation (SERVFAIL with CD=0
   but success with CD=1); a plain NXDOMAIN/outage is never mislabeled as a DNSSEC failure.
7. An **expired** apex RRSIG yields `infra.dnssec_rrsig_expiry` = `critical`; one expiring within the
   configured lead time (default 72h) yields `warning`.
8. `infra.dnssec_algorithm` flags algorithm 5 and 7 (RSASHA1) as `warning`; algorithm 13
   (ECDSAP256SHA256) and 8 (RSASHA256) pass.
9. `infra.dnssec_ds_algo_match` recomputes the DS SHA-256 digest locally and flags `critical` when no

### 9.2 Block 1 — What this is (concept copy + anchors)

Two-to-four paragraphs, plain language first (§1 is the source material): DNS answers are normally
unauthenticated; DNSSEC adds signatures (RRSIG) made with zone keys (DNSKEY) that a parent-published
digest (DS) anchors into a chain of trust from the root; a validating resolver proves the answer is
exactly what the zone owner published and sets AD=1. Then the two-state framing: **unsigned** is a
missing upgrade (advisory), **signed-but-broken** is an outage — validating resolvers SERVFAIL the
entire zone. Close with the DANE hook: DNSSEC is the hard prerequisite for
[`./dane_tlsa.mdx`](./dane_tlsa.mdx).

Anchored sub-headings. The first six are **required** by the [`./dns.mdx`](./dns.mdx) §14.6 map
(every underlined term app-wide routes here); the rest are local anchors this page's own copy and
finding rows link to:

| Anchor | Term | One-line content contract |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `#concept-dnssec` | DNSSEC | Signatures over DNS data; chain of trust root → TLD → zone; RFC 4033-4035. |
| `#concept-ds` | DS record | The parent-published digest of your KSK — the registrar step; no DS = island of security. |
| `#concept-dnskey` | DNSKEY | The zone's public keys; flags 257 = KSK, 256 = ZSK; algorithm number decides strength. |

### 9.6 Block 4 — What you can do (fix recipes)

Numbered steps, conditional lead-in per the fix-order ladder ([`./dns.mdx`](./dns.mdx) §8: broken

1. **Broken chain — DS mismatch / bogus / expired RRSIG (`critical`, fix today):**
   * Re-sign: `[copy]` `sudo rndc sign <domain>` (BIND) / re-enable the provider's auto-signing.
   * Republish the DS for the live KSK: `[copy]` `<domain>. IN DS <keyTag> <alg> 2 <sha256-digest>`
     — the page fills `<keyTag>`/`<alg>` from `dnskeyAlgos[]` and instructs pasting the digest from
     the DNS provider's DNSSEC panel into the registrar's DS form (digest type **2**, never 1).
   * Emergency fallback: `[copy]` registrar-ticket wording to **remove the DS** (going insecure
     beats staying bogus) with the re-add-after-re-sign caveat.
   * Verify: `[copy]` `delv +vtrace @8.8.8.8 <domain> MX` (look for `; fully validated`), then the
     block-5 spot-check.
2. **Island of security — signed, no DS (`warning`):** copy the DS/DNSKEY digest from the DNS
   provider into the registrar's DNSSEC panel; `[copy]` the exact DS line as above; enable

3. **Deprecated algorithm / SHA-1 DS / undersized RSA (`warning`):** the RFC 6781 roll plan —
   introduce the alg-13 key, dual-sign, wait ≥ 1 TTL, publish the new DS (type 2), retire the old
   key, remove the old DS; `[copy]` per-step commands.
4. **NSEC3 params (`warning`):** `[copy]` `NSEC3PARAM 1 0 0 -` (iterations 0, no opt-out, empty

5. **Unsigned (`info`):** enable DNSSEC at the provider (one-click at Cloudflare / Route 53 /

   the spot-check; framed as the DANE on-ramp.
6. **Deep checks pending (`info`):** enable `checks.dnssec.validateViaDig` in Settings so

## 12. Web-search grounding — how DNSSEC is tested, who judges it, and the correct values

*This section is the research spine the rest of the spec builds on. It answers three questions:
(a) how you actually test whether a domain's DNS is set up correctly for DNSSEC, (b) who checks
DNSSEC as part of anti-spam / deliverability and what they look at, and (c) exactly which values
must be present and correct. It supersedes nothing above — it grounds §2/§3/§13-§16.*

### 12.1 How DNSSEC is tested (the authoritative method)

DNSSEC is tested at two layers, and both must pass:

1. **Presence** — does the zone publish the four DNSSEC record types, and does the parent publish a
   matching **DS**? Fetch `DNSKEY`, `DS` (at the parent), `RRSIG`, and `NSEC/NSEC3`. Presence alone
   proves *nothing* about correctness — a zone can be signed and still be bogus.
2. **Validation** — does a *validating resolver* actually accept the chain and set the **AD
   (Authenticated Data)** flag? This is the only real pass/fail. `dig` **does not validate**; it only
   requests records and reports the `ad` bit that an upstream validating resolver set. To perform a
   true root→TLD→zone chain walk locally, use **`delv`** (BIND's validating resolver, shares BIND's
   validation code) or **`drill -TD`** (ldns). `delv` prints **`; fully validated`** on success.

The **bogus disambiguation** (the single most important measurement rule, already in §3): a bare
`SERVFAIL` is ambiguous. Query twice — once normally, once with **CD (Checking Disabled)**. If
`@1.1.1.1 <domain>` is `SERVFAIL` but `@1.1.1.1 +cd <domain>` **succeeds**, the data exists but its
signatures do not verify → **bogus** → `critical`. If both fail, it is an ordinary NXDOMAIN/outage,
never reported as a DNSSEC verdict.

Chain-of-trust inspection commands (the exact invocations §20 wires):
* `dig +dnssec DNSKEY <domain>` — the zone's keys + their RRSIGs.
* `dig DS <domain>` (asked at the parent) — the delegation digest that anchors trust.
* `dig +dnssec +cd <domain> SOA` and read `flags:` for `ad` — the validation signal.
* `dig DS <domain> +trace` — walk root → TLD → zone showing each DS hop.
* `delv +vtrace @8.8.8.8 <domain> MX` — full local validation with a verbose trace.
* `drill -TD <domain>` / `drill -S <domain>` — ldns chain trace / signature chase.

### 12.2 Who checks DNSSEC for spam / deliverability, and what they look at

DNSSEC is **not** a direct spam score at the big mailbox providers — Gmail, Yahoo, and Microsoft's
2024 bulk-sender rules mandate SPF, DKIM, DMARC, and low complaint rates, **not** DNSSEC. Its role in
deliverability is *indirect but load-bearing*, and several distinct parties evaluate it:

| Who | What they actually check | Consequence for the mail domain |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Validating recursive resolvers** (Google `8.8.8.8`, Cloudflare `1.1.1.1`, Quad9 `9.9.9.9`, Comcast, many ISPs — ~⅓ of the internet and most large mailbox providers' resolver paths) | Every DNS lookup for the domain: MX, SPF `TXT`, DKIM `TXT`, DMARC `_dmarc`, MTA-STS `TXT`. If the zone is **bogus**, they return `SERVFAIL` for all of them. | A broken chain is a **total outage** — inbound and outbound mail both stop, because auth-record lookups fail together. This is the highest-impact DNSSEC failure. |
| **DANE-aware receiving MTAs** (large European providers, many `.gov`/`.mil`, Postfix/Exim with DANE) | Look up **TLSA** — but *only* for DNSSEC-signed zones; treat a downgrade from a previously-seen TLSA as a hard fail. | Unsigned zone ⇒ DANE impossible; broken zone ⇒ DANE lookups fail. Governs whether opportunistic TLS is upgraded to authenticated TLS. |
| **Deliverability / audit tools** (checkdmarc, internet.nl, Hardenize, DNSViz, MxToolbox, Verisign DNSSEC debugger) | Grade the chain: signed?, DS present & matching?, algorithm modern?, RRSIGs current?, NSEC3 params sane?, validates? | Drive the "DNS hardening" / trust scores buyers and security teams read; a red DNSSEC grade dents domain reputation with humans, not spam filters. |
| **Registrar / registry policy & government mandates** (US M-21-31 / OMB, `.gov` requirements, some ccTLDs) | Whether regulated domains are signed at all. | Compliance, not spam — but relevant for the government/regulated senders in our persona set. |

**Bottom line for our severity model** (already encoded in §2): *unsigned* = `info` (missing upgrade,
no spam penalty), *broken* = `critical` (outage that takes every other auth record down with it),
*weak-but-working* = `warning`.

### 12.3 The values that must be present and correct

Grouped by record. These are the concrete "correct values" §15 (missing) and §16 (wrong) test
against, and the `dnssec_algorithms` seed (§5) encodes the algorithm policy.

**DNSKEY (the zone's public keys).**
* At least one key must exist (else *unsigned*).
* **Algorithm**: prefer **13 (ECDSAP256SHA256)** — the recommended default (RFC 8624/9904); **15
  (Ed25519)** also good; **14 (ECDSAP384SHA384)** and **16 (Ed448)** acceptable; **8 (RSASHA256)**
  acceptable when RSA ≥ 2048-bit; **5 (RSASHA1)** and **7 (RSASHA1-NSEC3-SHA1)** are **deprecated** →
  `warning`; algorithms **1/3/6/12** and **0** are forbidden/reserved.
* **Flags**: `257` = KSK (Key-Signing Key), `256` = ZSK (Zone-Signing Key). The DS must point at a KSK.
* **Key size** (RSA only): KSK/ZSK ≥ **2048-bit**; < 2048 → `warning`. ECDSA/EdDSA sizes are fixed.
* No large set of **stale keys** lingering after a rollover (bloats responses; signals abandonment).

**DS (delegation signer, published at the parent via the registrar).**
* At least one DS must exist for a signed zone (else *island of security* → `warning`).
* **Digest type**: **2 (SHA-256)** required; **1 (SHA-1)** deprecated → `warning`; **4
  (SHA-384)** acceptable; **0/3 (GOST)** not used.
* **Key Tag + Algorithm** must reference a live DNSKEY (KSK). Recompute `SHA256(owner | DNSKEY_RDATA)`
  (RFC 4034 §5.1.4) and confirm it equals the published DS digest for that key tag. No match after a
  key change ⇒ chain broken → `critical`.
* *(Worked example — the values a registrar DS form takes, from a real Namecheap/AWS handoff: Key Tag
  `21992`, Algorithm `13` (ECDSAP256SHA256), Digest Type `2` (SHA-256), Digest
  `F90B65…5D4EC9`. We never hard-code any domain's specific values — they are read live from the
  zone's DNSKEY and the parent DS. This is illustrative of the shape only.)*

**RRSIG (signatures over each RRset).**
* Every mail-relevant RRset (SOA, MX, and the SPF/DKIM/DMARC/MTA-STS TXT records) must carry an RRSIG,
  not just the apex.
* **Signature Expiration** (`YYYYMMDDHHMMSS`) must be in the future by more than the lead time
  (default **72h**). Already expired ⇒ `critical`; within lead time ⇒ `warning`. Typical validity is
  ~2-4 weeks with automatic re-signing.
* Signature Inception must be in the past (clock-skew tolerant).

**NSEC / NSEC3 (authenticated denial of existence).**
* NSEC3 **iterations must be 0** (RFC 9276); > 0 → `warning` (excess CPU; some resolvers treat high
  counts as insecure). Salt short or empty. **Opt-out** off unless the zone is a large delegation-heavy
  registry.

**Chain / validation (the end-to-end verdict).**
* A validating resolver returns **AD=1** and does not go **bogus**. This is the pass/fail that
  subsumes all the above at runtime.

# config.yaml  →  checks.dnssec
checks:

**(b) The per-run captured values — the `dns_infra.dnssec` block inside each run YAML**
(`~/.email_delivery_hero/runs/<domain>/<timestamp>.yaml`, [`../storage.mdx`](../storage.mdx),
[`./dns.mdx`](./dns.mdx) §5). This is the *observed-values* snapshot: exactly what the Brew tools
found, what was missing, and whether each value is correct. The shape is `DnssecResults` (§5); the
capture rules:

```yaml
# runs/<domain>/<timestamp>.yaml  →  dns_infra.dnssec
dns_infra:
  dnssec:
    signed: true                 # DNSKEY present at apex (else the zone is unsigned)
    dsPresent: true              # DS at parent (null if the DS lookup itself failed → "unknown", not "missing")
    validates: true              # AD=1 (null when deep path off / resolver unreachable — never false-critical)
    bogus: false                 # true only after the CD-flag disambiguation
    dnskeyAlgos:                 # one row per live key: what is there + whether it is correct
      - { keyTag: 21992, flags: 257, alg: 13, algName: ECDSAP256SHA256, bits: 256 }
    algorithms: [13]
    dsRecords:                   # §11 addition — one row per published DS, present/correct per row
      - { keyTag: 21992, algorithm: 13, digestType: 2 }
    dsDigestType: 2              # 2=SHA-256 (correct); 1=SHA-1 (deprecated → warning)
    dsAlgoMatch: true            # recomputed digest matches a live KSK (false ⇒ chain broken → critical)
    nsec3: true
    nsec3Iterations: 0           # must be 0 (RFC 9276); >0 → warning
    nsec3Optout: false
    rrsigEarliestExpiry: 2026-07-14T00:00:00Z   # soonest apex RRSIG expiry (null if not captured this run)
    rrsigs:                      # §11 deep-path addition — which RRset expires when
      - { typeCovered: SOA, keyTag: 12345, expiration: 2026-07-14T00:00:00Z }
    resolverUsed: "1.1.1.1"
    checkedAt: 2026-07-01T10:05:00Z
    toolsUsed: ["dig", "delv"]   # which Brew tools actually ran this round (provenance)
```

**Capture semantics (the rules that make "present / missing / correct / incorrect" unambiguous):**
* A field is **present** when the tool returned it and it parsed. A field the tool could not gather
  (deep path off, resolver timeout, DS lookup `SERVFAIL`) is written **`null`**, and the UI renders
  **"not captured this run"** — never a false negative (§9.4).
* **Missing vs. wrong** is a semantic distinction the *findings* carry, not the raw capture: `signed:
  false` → the `dnssec_signed.unsigned` **missing** finding; `dsAlgoMatch: false` on a signed zone →
  the `dnssec_ds_algo_match.mismatch` **wrong** finding (§15 vs §16).
* The **raw** tool output (the exact `dig`/`delv` stdout lines) is *not* stored in this parsed block —
  it lives in the run's `tool_runs[]` trail (§20.3), so the explainer's raw pane cites provenance
  without re-querying DNS.

## 15. Detecting MISSING DNSSEC settings

*User request "section 6": how we check when things are missing.* A "missing" condition is an
**absence** — the zone or the parent does not publish a required record. Missing is generally the
*advisory / warning* side of the model (the exception: a DS that references a key which is now missing
is a broken chain — that is a §16 "wrong" case, not a §15 "missing" case).

| Missing condition | How detected | Finding id | Severity | Remediation carried |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Zone not signed (no DNSKEY at apex) | `dig(domain,'DNSKEY')` empty / NODATA | `dnssec_signed.unsigned` | `info` | Enable DNSSEC at the DNS provider (one-click at Cloudflare/Route 53/Google Cloud DNS). |
| No DS at the parent, but the zone is signed (*island of security*) | `dig(domain,'DS')` empty **while** `signed=true` | `dnssec_ds_present.missing` | `warning` | Copy the DS/DNSKEY digest from the DNS provider into the registrar's DNSSEC panel (digest type 2). |
| An RRset returns without an RRSIG on a "signed" zone (partial signing) | `dig +dnssec <type>` returns records but no accompanying RRSIG | `dnssec_soa_signed.partial` | `warning` | Re-sign the full zone so the signer covers every RRset, not just the apex. |
| No RRSIG present at all on a signed zone (deep path) | `dig +dnssec DNSKEY` returns no RRSIG | `dnssec_rrsig_expiry.no_rrsig` | `warning` | Signer is not producing signatures — restart/repair the signing job. |
| DS present but references a key tag absent from the DNSKEY set (the KSK is *missing*) | recompute: no live DNSKEY matches the published DS key tag | `dnssec_chain_complete.broken` | **critical** | Re-align the parent DS with the live KSK (this is a *broken* chain — treated as §16 severity even though a key is "missing"). |
| DANE not possible because the zone is unsigned | derived: `signed=false` | `dnssec_dane_ready.signed`/`.not_validating` | `info` | Complete DNSSEC first, then publish TLSA ([`./dane_tlsa.mdx`](./dane_tlsa.mdx)). |
| Deep-path values not gathered (validation/RRSIG/NSEC3/chain) because the deep toggle is off | `validateViaDig=false` | `dnssec_*.pending` | `info` | Enable `checks.dnssec.validateViaDig` in Settings (§4). |
| A lookup transiently failed (no snapshot written) | the checker returns findings with **no** `results` payload | `dnssec_signed.unavailable` | `info` | Retry — the run could not determine DNSSEC state (never rendered as "unsigned", §9.4). |

**Rule:** *missing ≠ broken.* A never-signed zone is `info`; a signed-but-incomplete publication
(no DS, partial signing) is `warning`; only a *dangling reference* (DS → absent key) escalates to
`critical` and is reported through the §16 broken-chain machinery.

## 16. Detecting WRONG DNSSEC settings

*User request "section 7": how we check when settings are wrong.* A "wrong" condition is a record
that **exists but is incorrect** — deprecated, mismatched, expired, or misconfigured. Wrong conditions
are the `warning`/`critical` heart of the check because a wrong setting on a *signed* zone can take the
whole domain dark.

| Wrong condition | How detected | Finding id | Severity |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Deprecated signing algorithm (RSASHA1 = 5, or 7) | DNSKEY `algorithm` ∈ {5,7} vs the `algorithms` seed `deprecated:true` | `dnssec_algorithm.deprecated` | `warning` |
| Undersized RSA key (< 2048-bit) or stale-key bloat | parse DNSKEY bits / count | `dnssec_key_rollover.weak` | `warning` |
| DS digest type is SHA-1 (type 1) | `dsDigestType == 1` | `dnssec_ds_algo_match.sha1` | `warning` |
| **DS matches no live KSK** (recomputed SHA-256 ≠ any published DS) — botched rollover | `node:crypto` DS recompute vs published DS | `dnssec_ds_algo_match.mismatch` / `dnssec_chain_complete.broken` | **critical** |
| **Bogus** — signatures do not verify | `@1.1.1.1` SERVFAIL **and** `@1.1.1.1 +cd` success (deep path) | `dnssec_validates.bogus` | **critical** |
| No AD flag though the zone is signed (resolver did not validate) | `dig +dnssec` header `flags:` lacks `ad`, not bogus | `dnssec_validates.no_ad` | `warning`→`info` |
| **Expired** apex RRSIG | RRSIG `Signature Expiration` < now | `dnssec_rrsig_expiry.expired` | **critical** |
| RRSIG expiring within lead time (default 72h) | expiry < now + `rrsigLeadHours` | `dnssec_rrsig_expiry.near` | `warning` |
| NSEC3 iterations > 0 (or opt-out set inappropriately) | parse NSEC3PARAM | `dnssec_nsec3.params` | `warning` |

**The disambiguation guardrails (never mislabel):**
* **Bogus only after CD probe** — a bare SERVFAIL is never reported as bogus (§3, §8 AC#6).
* **Expiry only with a trusted clock** — clock skew can false-flag; validation math uses a trusted
  time source (§3 edge cases).
* **DS mismatch tolerates rollover** — during a key roll multiple keys are live; `ds_algo_match` must
  match **any** live KSK before flagging a mismatch (§3 edge cases).
* Every wrong-setting finding carries a **concrete** remediation (the exact DS line to republish, the
  exact re-sign command, the exact `NSEC3PARAM 1 0 0 -` value) — never "check your DNS" (§8 AC#10).

## 19. New DNSSEC pages — plan

*User request: which pages are new, and plan them out.* DNSSEC's two outage-shaped risks — a broken
chain (instant, domain-wide) and RRSIG expiry (a silent countdown) — are **cross-domain** concerns
that the per-domain explainer cannot surface. Two new fleet-level pages fill that gap. Both are
optional/roll-up views layered on data the run store already holds — no new checker work.

### 19.1 NEW — DNSSEC fleet view (`/dnssec`)
**Purpose:** one board answering "which of my domains are DNSSEC-healthy?" across the whole fleet.
**Data:** newest run per domain → `results["infra.dnssec"]`. **Layout:** one table, one row per domain:

| Domain | Signed | DS at parent | Validates (AD) | Algorithm | Soonest RRSIG expiry | State |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Row state = worst `infra.dnssec_*` for that domain (green/blue/amber/red dot). Sort: broken first,
then near-expiry, then unsigned, then healthy (mirrors the §2 severity ladder). Each row links to that
domain's DNSSEC explainer. Header summary chips: *N signed · N validate · N broken · N unsigned*.
Filter: state, "signed only", "broken only". This is the DNSSEC analogue of the blacklists §17 fleet
dashboard ([`./blacklists.mdx`](./blacklists.mdx)).

### 19.2 NEW — RRSIG expiry radar (`/dnssec/expiry`)
**Purpose:** the early-warning board for the silent-march-to-outage failure mode — a stalled
re-signing job. **Data:** every signed domain's `rrsigEarliestExpiry` (and, once §11's `rrsigs[]`
lands, per-RRset expiry). **Layout:** a list sorted ascending by days-to-expiry, with a **days-left
badge** (red < lead time, amber < 7d, green otherwise) and a small days-to-expiry sparkline (§11
trend). Rows past-due pin to the top with the "expired — zone bogus" banner. A single **"Re-check
expiring"** button re-runs the dnssec family for all domains under the lead-time threshold. This page
is the reason DNSSEC is a high-value scheduled check (§6).

### 19.3 Navigation & reachability
Both pages live under a top-level **"DNSSEC"** nav entry (leftbar, [`../leftbar.mdx`](../leftbar.mdx))
with the fleet view as the default tab and the expiry radar as its second tab. They are reachable from:
the Dashboard DNS & Infrastructure column header ("see all DNSSEC ›"), each domain explainer's "compare
across domains ›" link, and the RRSIG-expiry finding's "see all expiring ›" cross-link. Both degrade to
an empty state ("No domains signed yet") when the fleet has no signed zones.

## 20. Running the tools & background-processing integration

*User request: how we run the Brew/npm tools for the DNS checks — inputs, outputs, and integration
with background processing — and ensuring the background sync includes these DNS checks.*

### 20.1 The run funnel (who triggers a DNSSEC run)
Per [`./run_checks.mdx`](./run_checks.mdx) and the audit controller, DNSSEC runs on four triggers, all
converging on the same checker:
* **On-demand full audit** — `POST /api/audit/run/:domainId` runs the whole checker set including
  `infra.dnssec` as part of the DNS & Infrastructure pass.
* **DNS-category scoped** — `POST /api/audit/run/:domainId/check/dns` runs all ten `infra.*` families.
* **DNSSEC-family scoped (the block-5 spot-check)** — `POST
  /api/audit/run/:domainId/check/dns/dnssec` runs **only** `infra.dnssec`, writes a run tagged
  `run.scope: dns.dnssec` (§9.7).
* **Non-persisted spot-check** — `POST /api/audit/spot-check/:domainId/dnssec` for a live re-run that
  is not written to history.

### 20.2 Tool invocation — inputs and outputs
The checker's own runner is `dns-util.ts` (it calls `execFile` directly, **not** the generic
`tool-runner.ts`, so it can tag the `tool_runs[]` trail):

| Step | Invocation (input) | Output consumed |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Presence: signed | `dig DNSKEY <domain>` (`DNSKEY` is not a `node:dns` resolver type) | key list → `signed`, `dnskeyAlgos[]`, `algorithms[]` |
| Presence: DS | `dig DS <domain>` | DS list → `dsPresent`, `dsRecords[]`, `dsDigestType` |
| Local DS match | `node:crypto` recompute vs published DS | `dsAlgoMatch`, chain-complete verdict |
| Deep: validate | `digDnssec(domain,'SOA',{resolver})` + `{cd:true}` probe | `adFlag` → `validates`; SERVFAIL-vs-CD → `bogus` |
| Deep: RRSIG expiry | `dig +dnssec DNSKEY <domain>` | RRSIG `Signature Expiration` → `rrsigEarliestExpiry`, `rrsigs[]` |
| Deep: NSEC3 | `dig +dnssec <random>.<domain>` | NSEC3PARAM → `nsec3`, `nsec3Iterations`, `nsec3Optout` |
| 2nd opinion (optional) | `delv +vtrace @<res> <domain> MX` / `dnsviz probe … \| grok` / `checkdmarc` | corroborating validation verdict (evidence only) |

Every invocation is recorded as a `tool_runs[]` entry (tool, exact command, `started_at`,
`duration_ms`, `exit_code`, parsed output) tagged with `check_id = infra.dnssec_*`, so the explainer's
evidence lines cite real provenance and never re-query DNS at render (§9.4, §3.1).

### 20.3 Concurrency, cost, back-off
DNS is cheap and free; the only external touch is the public validating resolvers. Per
[`./run_checks.mdx`](./run_checks.mdx) global semaphores, DNSSEC queries share the DNS concurrency
budget; validation is **cached one-per-domain-per-run**, gently rate-limited, and **backs off** on
resolver timeout — reporting `info` "validation unavailable" rather than a false `critical` (§8 AC#5).

### 20.4 Background-processing integration (the scheduled sync)
The in-process scheduler ([`../scheduled_checks.mdx`](../scheduled_checks.mdx), launchd trigger
`code/deploy/launchd/trigger-scheduler.mjs`) **must include** `infra.dnssec` in every periodic pass —
it already does, because the scheduler runs the full `CHECKERS` set. DNSSEC-specific scheduling rules:
* **Default cadence** every 6h; **tighten to hourly** for any domain currently flagged near-expiry
  (`dnssec_rrsig_expiry.near`) — the periodic run is what catches a stalled re-signing job at
  `warning` *before* it becomes a `critical` outage (§6).
* Each periodic run **diffs against the previous** (§6 regression detection); a transition to
  bogus / DS-mismatch / expired is surfaced as a new problem on the Dashboard and (if new+critical)
  raises the enlarged progress toast (§18.8).
* The RRSIG expiry radar (§19.2) reads exactly this scheduled data; no separate job.

## 21. Storage — the DNSSEC run/batch growth

*User request: the values stored in storage when we run the checks — we check everything for a domain
in one run/pass, then save a batch; define exactly the new DNSSEC growth.* This section is the
storage-authority delta for DNSSEC; [`../storage.mdx`](../storage.mdx) and [`./dns.mdx`](./dns.mdx) §5
remain authoritative for paths, filenames, and the `run:` envelope — never redefined here.

### 21.1 Where the batch lives
One audit **run** = one YAML file at
`~/.email_delivery_hero/runs/<domain>/<timestamp>.yaml`, holding the whole domain's pass. DNSSEC's
slice is the **`dns_infra.dnssec`** block (= `results["infra.dnssec"]`), whose shape is the
`DnssecResults` snapshot documented in §5 and captured field-by-field in §13.2(b). Runs are also
projected into `runs.json` (the Dashboard history / newest-N index, [`../reports.mdx`](../reports.mdx)).

### 21.2 The DNSSEC growth — exactly what is added per run
Within a run's `dns_infra` section, DNSSEC contributes:
* **`dnssec:`** — the parsed `DnssecResults` snapshot (all fields in §13.2(b), including the §11
  additions `dsRecords[]` and, deep-path, `rrsigs[]`).
* **findings** — the 11 sub-check findings (`infra.dnssec_*`) merged into the section's `tests[]`
  projection with per-finding severity/title/detail/remediation.
* **`status`** — the family's worst-severity roll-up (drives the family chip and the §19 fleet state).
* **`tool_runs[]`** — the tagged provenance trail (§20.2) for every `dig`/`delv` call this run made,
  filtered to `check_id` starting `infra.` and stored at `dns_infra.tool_runs[]`.

### 21.3 Cross-run / batch-level derived data (no new storage)
The two new fleet pages (§19) and the trend glyphs derive entirely from existing per-run snapshots —
**no new files**:
* **Fleet view** — newest run per domain → `dns_infra.dnssec` (signed/ds/validates/algorithm/expiry).
* **Expiry radar** — every signed domain's `rrsigEarliestExpiry` (+ `rrsigs[]` once recorded) across
  newest runs, sorted ascending.
* **Trend / change markers** — the §16.3/§11 field set (`signed`, `algorithms[]`, `ds_digest_types[]`,
  `ds_matches_dnskey`; requested additions `rrsigEarliestExpiry`, `validates`) diffed against the most
  recent earlier run whose scope included the dnssec family.

### 21.4 Migration note
When storage graduates to PostgreSQL (§5), `dns_infra.dnssec` → one `dnssec_check_results` row keyed
by `(audit_run_id, domain_id)`, the `config.yaml` algorithm seed → the `dnssec_algorithms` table, and
`tool_runs[]` → the shared tool-run table. No shape change to `findings`. The field names in §13.2(b)
are chosen to make that mapping mechanical.

## 22. UX designer — detailed UI layout, components, and controls

*Second follow-up request: as a UX designer, specify exactly how every DNSSEC surface is laid out, the
UI components, and the controls. This section is the build-ready component spec; it does not change the
information design (§4, §9, §17-§19) — it dresses it. Stack: React 19 + Vite, **shadcn/ui**
components, **lucide-react** icons, Tailwind. Every component named below already exists in the
frontend design system unless marked NEW.*

### 22.1 Shared DNSSEC visual language (use everywhere)
* **Severity → color + icon** (one mapping, no exceptions): `ok` → emerald + `ShieldCheck`; `info` →
  slate/blue + `ShieldQuestion`; `warning` → amber + `ShieldAlert`; `critical` → red + `ShieldOff`.
  Rendered as a shadcn `Badge` (`variant` mapped to severity) with the lucide icon at 16px left of the
  label. **Never** use the generic check/x icons for DNSSEC — the shield family reads "security" at a
  glance.
* **The "outage" accent** — any `critical` DNSSEC surface (bogus / expired / DS-mismatch) gets a
  left border-accent (`border-l-4 border-red-600`) and, at page/section top, an `Alert`
  (`variant="destructive"`) titled **"Domain may be unresolvable"** with an `AlertTriangle` icon.
* **Copy affordance** — every publishable artifact (a DS line, an `NSEC3PARAM` value, a verify
  command) sits in a monospace `<code>` block with a trailing ghost `Button` (`size="icon"`,
  `Clipboard` icon → `ClipboardCheck` for 1.2s on click). This is the single most-used control on
  these pages; it must be visually consistent everywhere.
* **"Deep check off" band** — a single muted `Alert` (`variant="default"`, `Info` icon) that replaces
  the four `.pending` rows, with an inline `Button variant="link"` → Settings.

### 22.2 Category-page DNSSEC family group (`/domains/:id/dns`)
* Container: shadcn `Accordion` item, header = **"DNSSEC"** + family StatusCell badge + a right-aligned
  `details ›` `Button variant="ghost"` (→ explainer).
* Body: a `TestResultsTable` (existing) of the 11 sub-test rows. Columns: severity badge · title ·
  observed detail (truncate w/ tooltip) · remediation · evidence chevron. Fail rows **pre-expanded**
  (the `Collapsible` open by default when severity ≥ warning).
* Row expansion reveals the evidence panel: the `tool_runs[]` line as a `dig · 187 ms` chip →
  monospace output, plus the copy-able remediation artifact.
* When deep path off: the four validation rows collapse into the §22.1 "Deep check off" band placed
  where those rows would be, preserving §2 order for the remaining seven.

### 22.3 Check-detail explainer (`…/dns/check/dnssec`) — the five-block layout (§9)
A single scrollable column, `max-w-3xl`, blocks stacked with `space-y-8`; a sticky right-rail TOC
(`hidden lg:block`) lists the five blocks + the §9.2 concept anchors.

* **Block 1 — What this is.** `Card` with `prose` copy (§9.2). Each concept term is an underlined
  `<a href="#concept-…">`; the anchored sub-headings render as `<h3 id="concept-…">` with a hover
  anchor-link icon. Renders statically (before any run data).
* **Block 2 — Is it healthy right now?** Header row: the family StatusCell + one verdict sentence
  (worst-state-first, §9.3). If `critical`, the §22.1 destructive `Alert` banner sits above it. Body:
  the same 11-row `TestResultsTable` as §22.2 but **run-qualified**, fail rows pre-expanded. Below it,
  the **raw + parsed** frame as two shadcn `Tabs` ("Parsed" default, "Raw"): Parsed = the §9.4
  field table with a per-field `Badge` verdict chip and **"not captured this run"** muted text for
  nulls; Raw = monospace `tool_runs[]` panes each with a copy button and a `dig · 187 ms` provenance
  chip. Never re-queries at render.
* **Block 3 — What it means.** `Card` whose body is chosen by observed worst-state (§9.5) — one of four
  copy variants (rejected-now / eroding / hardening-available / healthy). The `critical` variant leads
  with a red inline callout.
* **Block 4 — What you can do.** An ordered `<ol>` of fix recipes (§9.6), conditional lead-in per the
  fix-order ladder. Each numbered step ends in the §22.1 copy control carrying the exact artifact (the
  filled-in `DS` line, `sudo rndc sign …`, `NSEC3PARAM 1 0 0 -`, `delv +vtrace …`). No prose-only step.
* **Block 5 — Run this check now.** A `Card` with a primary `Button` ("Run DNSSEC check") that POSTs
  the family spot-check (§9.7). Button copy states scope + cost (*"Runs DNSSEC only — DNS queries,
  ~2 s; deep path adds +dnssec probes, ~5-10 s"*) and the last-run timestamp. State machine (§9.7):
  idle → running (`Loader2` spin, disabled) → done (diff-flash of changed rows, green pulse) → error
  (`Alert`); disabled with an inline reason (tooltip) while any run for the domain is in flight (409).
* **Footer** — the history strip (`HistoryStrip`, small severity dots per prior run, hover =
  timestamp+verdict) and the references frame (§9.8 rows as a compact link list), plus the
  since-last-run trend glyph (▲/▼/●) on `signed`/`algorithms[]`/`ds_digest_types[]`/`ds_matches_dnskey`.

### 22.4 Settings — DNSSEC admin card (`/settings/checks` → DNSSEC) (§18.7)
A shadcn `Card` titled **"DNSSEC"** (with `ShieldCheck` icon), three `Form` fields stacked
`space-y-6`, one primary `Button` "Save":
* **Validating resolvers** — a chips/tag input (`Input` + removable `Badge` chips), default chips

  *"Resolvers the deep check queries for the AD flag."*
* **RRSIG near-expiry lead time** — a numeric `Input type="number"` with a "hours" suffix adornment,
  default `72`, min 1. Helper: *"Warn this many hours before an RRSIG expires."*
* **Deep validation (`dig +dnssec`)** — a shadcn `Switch` with label **"Run validation, RRSIG-expiry,
  NSEC3 & chain sub-checks"**, default **off**; when off, a muted helper reads *"These four sub-checks
  stay pending until enabled."*
Unsaved edits enable Save + show a `Badge variant="outline"` "Unsaved". Save → toast "DNSSEC settings
saved", persists to `config.yaml → checks.dnssec`.

### 22.5 NEW — DNSSEC fleet view (`/dnssec`) (§19.1)
* Page header: title **"DNSSEC"** + subtitle, and a summary chip row (`Badge` set): *N signed · N
  validate · N broken · N unsigned*, each chip a filter toggle.
* A shadcn `Tabs` bar: **"Fleet"** (default) | **"Expiry radar"** (§22.6).
* Body (Fleet): a `DataTable` (existing) — columns Domain · Signed · DS · Validates · Algorithm ·
  Soonest RRSIG expiry · State. Boolean cells render a small `ShieldCheck`/`ShieldOff` glyph, not
  text; State = the severity dot. Default sort broken→near-expiry→unsigned→healthy. Row click →
  that domain's explainer. Column headers sortable; a `Select` filter for state; a search `Input` for
  domain. Empty state: an `EmptyState` card "No domains signed yet — enable DNSSEC to see them here."
* Density: comfortable rows, sticky header, zebra off (severity dot carries the scanning weight).

### 22.6 NEW — RRSIG expiry radar (`/dnssec/expiry`) (§19.2)
Second tab of §22.5. A vertical list (`<ol>`), each item a `Card`-lite row:
* left: domain + its worst DNSSEC state dot;
* center: a **days-left `Badge`** (red < lead time, amber < 7d, emerald otherwise) and the absolute
  expiry timestamp;
* right: a small days-to-expiry `Sparkline` (once §11 `rrsigs[]`/trend lands) and a per-row `Button
  variant="ghost"` "Re-check".

Empty state: "Nothing expiring — all signed zones have healthy signature windows."

### 22.7 Progress toast (global) (§18.8)
Reuse the enlarged bottom-left toast ([`../progress_ui.mdx`](../progress_ui.mdx)); DNSSEC critical
variant: `ShieldOff` icon, red accent, title *"<domain>: DNSSEC chain broken"*, body *"may be

### 22.8 Accessibility & responsive rules
* Severity is **never** conveyed by color alone — the shield icon shape + the badge text carry it for
  color-blind users; verdict chips include a `sr-only` word ("passed"/"deprecated"/"broken").
* All tables scroll inside an `overflow-x-auto` container on narrow viewports; the explainer's
  right-rail TOC collapses below `lg`.
* Copy buttons have `aria-label="Copy <artifact name>"`; the run-now button announces state changes via
  an `aria-live="polite"` region.
* Focus order follows visual order; the destructive `Alert` is `role="alert"` so it is announced
  immediately when a critical state loads.
