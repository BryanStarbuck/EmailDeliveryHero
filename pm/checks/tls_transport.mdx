# STARTTLS Transport Encryption & MX TLS Certificate Health

Modern receivers increasingly demand that inbound mail arrive over TLS, and the 2024 Gmail/Yahoo
bulk-sender rules push senders to encrypt in transit. **STARTTLS** ([RFC 3207](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3207))
is the SMTP extension that upgrades a plaintext connection on port 25 to TLS after `EHLO`. This check
**connects to each of a domain's MX hosts, issues `EHLO`, looks for the `250-STARTTLS` capability,
performs the TLS handshake, and inspects the certificate and negotiated parameters**. A missing
STARTTLS advertisement, an expired or hostname-mismatched certificate, a self-signed or
broken-chain certificate, or a downgrade to SSLv3/TLS 1.0 all mean mail can be delivered in the
clear or with an untrustworthy cert — receivers that enforce TLS (via **MTA-STS** or **DANE**) will
then **defer or bounce** your inbound mail, and your outbound TLS reputation suffers.

This is **opportunistic-TLS hygiene**: it measures whether TLS is *offered and healthy*. It does
**not** measure whether TLS is *enforced* — enforcement is the job of MTA-STS and DANE, covered in
their own specs. A domain can pass every sub-check here and still be trivially downgradable by an
active attacker unless MTA-STS `enforce` or DANE/TLSA is also published.

## 1. What it checks

**The standard.** SMTP is plaintext by default ([RFC 5321](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc5321)).
[RFC 3207](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3207) defines the `STARTTLS` service extension: after a
client sends `EHLO`, a TLS-capable server advertises `250-STARTTLS`; the client issues the
`STARTTLS` command, the server replies `220 Ready to start TLS`, and both sides run a TLS handshake
before the session continues with a fresh `EHLO`. Everything after the handshake — envelope,
headers, body — is encrypted.

**Why receivers care.** Opportunistic STARTTLS protects mail from passive interception on the wire.
Large receivers report TLS-negotiation outcomes back via TLS-RPT and increasingly gate delivery on
it. If your MX cannot complete a clean handshake — no STARTTLS, expired cert, wrong hostname, broken
chain, or an ancient protocol — a sender that has committed to TLS (because you publish an MTA-STS
`enforce` policy or DANE TLSA records) has no safe way to deliver and will **defer then bounce**.
Even without enforcement, weak transport TLS is a negative signal that suppresses inbox placement.

**The common real-world failure mode.** A certificate silently expires (nobody renewed the MX
cert), or the MX host presents a cert for the *mail platform's* hostname (e.g. `mail.provider.net`)
while your MX record points at `mx.yourdomain.com` and you also publish MTA-STS listing
`mx.yourdomain.com` — so the name never matches under `enforce`. Self-signed certs and
missing-intermediate chains are the next most common: the handshake completes but no public CA
trusts it, so DANE-`PKIX-TA`/MTA-STS validation fails.

**How a failure degrades inbox placement.** No STARTTLS → mail in clear, TLS-enforcing receivers
refuse. Expired/mismatched/untrusted cert → MTA-STS `enforce` and DANE validation fail → inbound
deferrals/bounces and outbound TLS-reputation loss. Legacy protocol/cipher → downgrade exposure and
receiver distrust. All of it caps deliverability quietly, because a plaintext delivery still
"works" — until a receiver stops accepting it.

## 2. Sub-checks

Each MX host discovered via [./mx_routing.mdx](./mx_routing.mdx) is probed independently; findings
are emitted **per MX host** (the `detail` names the host and IP). Worst severity across all hosts
rolls into the `infra` dashboard cell.

| Sub-check (checkId) | What it verifies | How measured | Typical critical / warning finding | Concrete remediation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `infra.starttls_offered` | Each MX advertises `STARTTLS` in the `EHLO` response. | SMTP connect :25 → `EHLO edh.local` → scan for `250-STARTTLS`. | **critical:** MX offers no STARTTLS (plaintext-only). | Enable the STARTTLS extension on the MTA (Postfix `smtpd_tls_security_level = may`; Exim `tls_advertise_hosts = *`) and install a cert. |
| `infra.tls_cert_valid` | Presented cert is within its validity window (`notBefore ≤ now ≤ notAfter`). | Read `notBefore`/`notAfter` from the handshake peer cert. | **critical:** cert expired or not-yet-valid. | Renew/replace the MX certificate so `notAfter` is in the future; automate renewal (e.g. certbot/ACME) and reload the MTA. |
| `infra.tls_cert_expiry_soon` | Cert expiry is not imminent (advisory runway). | `notAfter − now`; warn `< 14 days`, info `< 30 days`. | **warning:** cert expires in `< 14 days`. | Renew now and switch to automated ACME renewal so certs roll ≥ 30 days before expiry. |
| `infra.tls_cert_name_match` | Cert CN/SAN matches the MX hostname being connected to. | Compare MX host against cert `subjectAltName` DNS entries (wildcards per RFC 6125), fall back to CN. | **critical:** MX host not in cert SAN/CN. | Issue a cert whose SAN includes the exact MX hostname (`mx.<domain>`); do not rely on the provider's default cert name. |
| `infra.tls_cert_chain` | Cert chains to a publicly trusted CA; no missing intermediates; not self-signed. | Verify against Node's bundled Mozilla CA store; detect self-signed / `unable to get local issuer`. | **critical:** self-signed or broken/incomplete chain (untrusted). | Install the full chain (leaf + intermediates) in the MTA cert file; replace self-signed certs with a CA-issued cert. |
| `infra.tls_version` | Negotiated protocol is ≥ TLS 1.2 (ideally 1.3). | Read `TLSSocket.getProtocol()`; also probe max supported version. | **critical:** SSLv3/TLS 1.0/1.1 negotiated or highest offered. | Disable SSLv3/TLS 1.0/1.1; set minimum to TLS 1.2 and enable TLS 1.3 (Postfix `smtpd_tls_protocols = >=TLSv1.2`). |
| `infra.tls_cipher` | Negotiated cipher is strong with forward secrecy; no RC4/3DES/export/NULL. | Read `getCipher()`/`getEphemeralKeyInfo()`; classify suite. | **critical:** RC4/3DES/EXPORT/NULL or non-PFS cipher. | Restrict to modern PFS suites (ECDHE-\* AEAD, `TLS_AES_128_GCM_SHA256`); remove RC4/3DES/export ciphers. |
| `infra.tls_downgrade` | The session is not forced/observed to fall back to cleartext or a weak version. | If `STARTTLS` advertised but handshake fails/plaintext succeeds where TLS should hold. | **warning:** STARTTLS advertised but handshake fails → server falls back to cleartext. | Fix the handshake failure; pair with MTA-STS `enforce`/DANE (see [./mta_sts.mdx](./mta_sts.mdx), [./dane_tlsa.mdx](./dane_tlsa.mdx)) so downgrade is refused. |
| `infra.tls_sni` | Server handles SNI: presents the correct cert when the MX hostname is sent as SNI. | Handshake sending `servername = <mx-host>`; compare cert vs a no-SNI handshake. | **warning:** wrong/default cert returned unless SNI sent, or SNI ignored. | Configure per-hostname TLS (SNI) on the MTA so `mx.<domain>` gets its matching cert. |
| `infra.tls_ocsp` *(advisory)* | Cert is not revoked; OCSP stapling offered. | Request OCSP staple during handshake; note presence/`good`/`revoked`. | **critical:** cert revoked. **info:** no OCSP staple. | Replace a revoked cert immediately; enable OCSP stapling on the MTA to speed revocation checks. |
| `infra.tls_key_strength` *(advisory)* | Public key meets modern strength (RSA ≥ 2048, ECDSA ≥ 256). | Read peer cert public-key type/bits. | **warning:** RSA < 2048 or weak key. | Reissue the cert with RSA ≥ 2048 or ECDSA P-256. |
| `infra.tls_sig_algorithm` *(advisory)* | Signature algorithm is not deprecated (no SHA-1/MD5). | Inspect cert `sigalg`. | **warning:** SHA-1-signed cert. | Reissue with SHA-256+ signature. |
| `infra.tls_consistency` *(advisory)* | All MX hosts present coherent TLS posture (no one host dragging the domain down). | Compare per-host results across the MX set. | **warning:** one MX host lacks STARTTLS/valid cert while others pass. | Bring the lagging MX host to parity (STARTTLS + valid, name-matching cert). |

> All rows in this spec are **network-probe** sub-checks — none are pure `node:dns/promises`. The
> only DNS this check does is reuse the MX host list from [./mx_routing.mdx](./mx_routing.mdx).

## 3. How it is measured

**Prerequisite (DNS).** Reuse the MX result from [./mx_routing.mdx](./mx_routing.mdx)
(`resolveMx(domain)` sorted by priority) and each MX host's A/AAAA. This check does **not** re-derive
MX; it consumes it. If the domain has no MX, this check is skipped with an `info` and defers to the
MX-routing finding.

**The probe (FUTURE round — TLS-aware SMTP).** For each MX host, on port 25:

1. TCP connect, read the `220` banner.
2. Send `EHLO edh.<local-fqdn>`; parse the multiline `250-` capability list. Absence of
   `250-STARTTLS` → `infra.starttls_offered` critical; stop (plaintext only).
3. Send `STARTTLS`; expect `220`. Anything else → `infra.tls_downgrade` warning.
4. Perform the TLS handshake with `servername = <mx-host>` (SNI), presenting our client hello with
   TLS 1.3+1.2 offered. Two implementation paths, both acceptable:
   - **Node built-in:** `net.connect` for SMTP, drive EHLO/STARTTLS as text, then
     `tls.connect({ socket, servername, rejectUnauthorized: false })` and read
     `socket.getPeerCertificate(true)`, `getProtocol()`, `getCipher()`, `getEphemeralKeyInfo()`,
     `getPeerX509Certificate()`. We set `rejectUnauthorized: false` so we can *report* trust
     problems instead of throwing, then evaluate trust ourselves.
   - **Shell to a Brew tool:** `openssl s_client -starttls smtp -connect <mx>:25 -servername <mx>`
     (parse `Verify return code`, `Protocol`, `Cipher`, cert `notBefore/notAfter`, SAN), or
     **`swaks --to probe@<domain> --server <mx> -tls`** for an end-to-end STARTTLS transcript. The
     project charter favors Brew tools; the `Checker` contract is unchanged — only the checker
     internals differ.
5. Extract from the leaf cert: `subject`, `issuer`, `notBefore`, `notAfter`, SAN DNS names, public
   key type/bits, `sigalg`, and (if stapled) the OCSP response status.

**Trust & name evaluation.**
- **Chain:** verify the presented chain against Node's bundled Mozilla root store. `self-signed
  certificate` / `unable to get local issuer certificate` → `infra.tls_cert_chain` critical. A
  handshake that only succeeds because we sent extra intermediates ourselves indicates a
  server-side missing-intermediate → still flag.
- **Name match:** apply RFC 6125 matching — exact SAN DNS entry, or left-most wildcard
  (`*.example.com` matches `mx.example.com` but not `example.com` or `a.b.example.com`). CN is a
  fallback only, and is ignored if any SAN is present.
- **Protocol/cipher:** map `getProtocol()` to severity (`TLSv1.3`/`TLSv1.2` ok; `TLSv1.1`/`TLSv1`
  critical; `SSLv3` critical). Classify the cipher: AEAD + ECDHE/DHE = ok; CBC-only = info; RC4,
  3DES, EXPORT, NULL, anon = critical; non-PFS (static RSA kx) = warning.

**Edge cases.** Port 25 blocked outbound from the app host (many ISPs) → probe cannot run; emit
`info` "TLS probe unavailable from this host" rather than a false critical, and surface a settings
hint (see §4). Greylisting/tarpitting → apply a connect timeout (default 10 s) and one retry.
IPv6-only MX where the host lacks IPv6 egress → fall back to A record. Multiple A records per MX
host → probe the first reachable, note the IP tested. A host that offers `STARTTLS` but resets on
the `STARTTLS` command → `infra.tls_downgrade` warning (broken TLS worse than honest cleartext for
enforcing senders).

**Caching / rate limits.** Probing costs a live SMTP+TLS handshake per MX host, so results are
cached per audit run (not per sub-check) — one handshake yields *all* `infra.tls_*` findings for
that host. Cap concurrency (e.g. 4 hosts in parallel) and back off per MX IP to avoid tripping
connection-rate limits on shared mail platforms. Never probe more than once per host per audit.

**Severity mapping (worst-wins per host, then per domain):** expired/mismatched/untrusted cert,
no STARTTLS, revoked cert, or SSLv3/TLS1.0/1.1/weak-cipher = **critical**; expiry `< 14 days`,
downgrade/handshake failure, SNI misbehavior, weak key/sig, host inconsistency = **warning**; no
OCSP staple, expiry `< 30 days`, probe-unavailable = **info**.

## 4. User interface

**Dashboard contribution.** These findings feed the **DNS & Infrastructure** column cell (shared
with MX, PTR, MTA-STS, etc. per [../dashboard.mdx](../dashboard.mdx)). A critical TLS finding on any
MX host turns the cell **dark red**; a warning-only TLS posture contributes **amber**; info alone
(no OCSP staple) does not change the color. The cell tooltip summarizes e.g. "2 MX hosts: 1 cert
expired".

**Audit-detail view.** Findings render as grouped rows under a **"Transport Security (TLS)"**
sub-group, one block per MX host, each row = severity badge + observed `detail` + `remediation` with
a **copy-to-clipboard** button (React 19 + shadcn/ui `Badge`, `Card`, `Button`, `lucide-react`
`ShieldCheck`/`ShieldAlert`/`Clock` icons; `Collapsible` per host). Certificate facts render in a
compact key/value panel.

```
┌ Transport Security (TLS) ─────────────────────────────── infra ┐
│  mx1.example.com  (203.0.113.10)                    ● critical │
│   ● critical  Certificate expired                              │
│      notAfter 2026-06-20 (11 days ago) · issuer Let's Encrypt  │
│      Fix: renew the MX cert (certbot renew) & reload  [copy]   │
│   ✔ ok        STARTTLS offered · TLS 1.3 · ECDHE-AES256-GCM    │
│      SAN mx1.example.com,mx2.example.com                        │
│  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────── │
│  mx2.example.com  (203.0.113.11)                    ● ok       │
│   ✔ ok  Valid cert · matches host · chain trusted · 71 days    │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Cert detail fields shown:** MX host, IP tested, negotiated protocol, cipher + PFS flag, subject,
issuer, SAN list, `notBefore`, `notAfter`, days-to-expiry, name-match bool, chain-trusted bool,
OCSP status.

**Per-domain config.** An admin-only **"TLS probe"** panel: toggle the probe on/off (for hosts where
port 25 is blocked), set the connect timeout and expiry-warning threshold (default 14/30 days), and
an optional EHLO name override. **Empty / never-run:** the cell renders light gray "Not yet
audited"; the TLS group shows "Run an audit to probe MX TLS." **Loading:** a skeleton per MX host
while handshakes run. **Probe-unavailable** (port 25 blocked): an inline `info` banner "TLS probe
could not connect from this host — outbound :25 may be blocked" with a link to the settings toggle.

## 5. Information schema (database schema)

Reference the shared core tables (owned by engineering, do **not** redefine): `domains`,
`sending_ips`, `dkim_selectors`, `audit_runs`, `findings`. Every emitted finding is a row in
`findings` with `category = 'infra'` and `check_id` = one of the `infra.tls_*` / `infra.starttls_*`
ids. This check adds one structured result table, keyed per MX host per audit run.

```sql
-- One row per MX host probed in an audit run.
CREATE TABLE tls_check_results (
  id              BIGSERIAL   PRIMARY KEY,
  audit_run_id    BIGINT      NOT NULL REFERENCES audit_runs(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
  domain_id       BIGINT      NOT NULL REFERENCES domains(id)    ON DELETE CASCADE,
  mx_host         TEXT        NOT NULL,             -- the MX hostname probed
  mx_ip           INET,                             -- the address actually connected to
  starttls_offered BOOLEAN    NOT NULL,             -- 250-STARTTLS advertised
  handshake_ok    BOOLEAN     NOT NULL,             -- TLS negotiation completed
  tls_version     TEXT,                             -- 'TLSv1.3' | 'TLSv1.2' | 'TLSv1.1' | ...
  cipher          TEXT,                             -- negotiated suite, e.g. 'ECDHE-RSA-AES256-GCM-SHA384'
  forward_secrecy BOOLEAN,                          -- ECDHE/DHE key exchange
  cert_subject    TEXT,
  cert_issuer     TEXT,
  cert_sans       TEXT[],                           -- SAN DNS names
  cert_not_before TIMESTAMPTZ,
  cert_not_after  TIMESTAMPTZ,
  cert_name_match BOOLEAN,                          -- SAN/CN matches mx_host (RFC 6125)
  chain_trusted   BOOLEAN,                          -- chains to a Mozilla root, not self-signed
  self_signed     BOOLEAN,
  key_type        TEXT,                             -- 'RSA' | 'EC'
  key_bits        INT,
  sig_algorithm   TEXT,                             -- e.g. 'sha256WithRSAEncryption'
  ocsp_status     TEXT,                             -- 'good' | 'revoked' | 'none'
  probe_error     TEXT,                             -- populated when handshake_ok = false
  checked_at      TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT now()
);

CREATE INDEX idx_tls_results_domain_host ON tls_check_results (domain_id, mx_host);
CREATE INDEX idx_tls_results_audit       ON tls_check_results (audit_run_id);
-- Fast "which certs expire soon" queries across all domains:
CREATE INDEX idx_tls_results_expiry      ON tls_check_results (cert_not_after);
```

**Mapping onto today's JSON file store.** There is **no database today** (see
[../engineering.mdx](../engineering.mdx) §7). Each audit run is a single file
`~/.email_delivery_hero/audits/<domainId>/<auditId>.json`. The `findings[]` array in that file
already holds the human-facing TLS findings. The structured `tls_check_results` rows are persisted
in the **same file** under a `checkData.tls` key — an array of per-MX-host objects whose fields are
exactly the columns above (camelCase), e.g.:

```jsonc
"checkData": {
  "tls": [
    { "mxHost": "mx1.example.com", "mxIp": "203.0.113.10", "starttlsOffered": true,
      "handshakeOk": true, "tlsVersion": "TLSv1.3", "cipher": "TLS_AES_256_GCM_SHA384",
      "forwardSecrecy": true, "certSubject": "CN=mx1.example.com", "certIssuer": "R11 / Let's Encrypt",
      "certSans": ["mx1.example.com","mx2.example.com"], "certNotBefore": "2026-03-01T00:00:00Z",
      "certNotAfter": "2026-06-20T00:00:00Z", "certNameMatch": true, "chainTrusted": true,
      "selfSigned": false, "keyType": "EC", "keyBits": 256, "sigAlgorithm": "ecdsa-with-SHA256",
      "ocspStatus": "good", "checkedAt": "2026-07-01T12:00:00Z" }
  ]
}
```

The `store` module owns this shape. When storage graduates to PostgreSQL, `checkData.tls[]`
maps one-to-one onto `tls_check_results` rows (join key: `audit_run_id` = the audit file's `id`,
`domain_id` = the folder's domain). No lookup/reference tables are required for this check — trust
anchors come from Node's bundled Mozilla CA store, not a DB table.

## 6. When we run the check

**On-demand.** Runs as part of a full audit: `POST /api/domains/:id/audit` (and the dashboard
"Run now"). Because it opens live SMTP+TLS handshakes, it is one of the slower checks; the runner
executes it in parallel with pure-DNS checkers but bounds MX-host concurrency to ~4.

**Periodic.** The in-process scheduler ([../scheduled_checks.mdx](../scheduled_checks.mdx)) re-runs it
on the configured cadence (default every 6 h). Certificate expiry makes periodic runs especially
valuable: the diff engine catches `infra.tls_cert_expiry_soon` crossing the 30-day and 14-day
thresholds and `infra.tls_cert_valid` flipping to expired **before** a receiver starts bouncing.
Consider a daily minimum cadence for TLS specifically so expiry is caught with runway.

**First-round vs future.** **FUTURE round.** The entire check depends on an outbound SMTP+TLS probe
(EHLO → STARTTLS → handshake → certificate inspection) via Node's `tls`/`net` or shelling to
`openssl s_client` / `swaks`. None of it runs on first-round `node:dns/promises`. Enable it once the
probe harness (and admin toggle for port-25-blocked hosts) ships. This is the enforcement-free
sibling of MTA-STS/DANE: it needs the same TLS machinery those checks build on.

**Cost / rate-limit / back-off.** One handshake per MX host per run; cache results per run so all
`infra.tls_*` sub-checks read a single handshake. Back off per MX IP; respect a connect timeout
(10 s) with a single retry; skip cleanly with `info` if outbound :25 is blocked. Never hammer a
shared mail platform — cap concurrency and honor `4xx`/rate replies.

**Regression detection.** The scheduler diff treats any newly-appearing or worsened TLS finding as a
"new problem": cert newly expired, chain that stopped validating, protocol downgraded, or a
previously-passing MX host that stopped offering STARTTLS all surface on the Dashboard as new
problems on the next tick.

## 8. Acceptance criteria

1. For a domain with healthy MX TLS (STARTTLS offered, valid CA cert, SAN matching the MX host,
   TLS 1.2/1.3, PFS cipher), the check emits only `ok`/`info` findings and does not turn the
   `infra` cell red or amber.
2. Given an MX host that does **not** advertise STARTTLS, `infra.starttls_offered` is `critical`
   with a remediation naming the exact MTA directive to enable STARTTLS.
3. Given an expired certificate, `infra.tls_cert_valid` is `critical` and `detail` shows `notAfter`
   and days-since-expiry; given a cert expiring in `< 14 days`, `infra.tls_cert_expiry_soon` is
   `warning`.
4. Given a cert whose SAN/CN does not include the MX hostname, `infra.tls_cert_name_match` is
   `critical` and lists the presented SAN names vs the expected MX host.
5. Given a self-signed cert or a chain missing intermediates, `infra.tls_cert_chain` is `critical`;
   a fully trusted chain yields `ok`.
6. Given a negotiated protocol below TLS 1.2 (or a highest-offered below 1.2),
   `infra.tls_version` is `critical`; a RC4/3DES/export/NULL/non-PFS cipher makes
   `infra.tls_cipher` `critical`.
7. The check **iterates every MX host** for the domain and emits per-host findings; the worst host's
   severity determines the domain's `infra` roll-up.
8. Every non-`ok` finding carries a concrete `remediation` (exact renewal command / MTA directive /
   cert reissue instruction) — never a generic "fix your TLS".
9. When outbound port 25 is blocked from the app host, the check emits a single `info`
   "probe unavailable" finding and does **not** produce false criticals.
10. One handshake per MX host per audit run populates all `infra.tls_*` findings and one
    `tls_check_results`-shaped object (persisted under `checkData.tls` in the audit JSON today).
11. The scheduler diff flags a cert that newly expired, a chain that stopped validating, or an MX
    host that stopped offering STARTTLS as a "new problem" on the next run.
12. The spec correctly distinguishes this **audit-only** check from the **enforcement** checks in
    [./mta_sts.mdx](./mta_sts.mdx) and [./dane_tlsa.mdx](./dane_tlsa.mdx), and reuses the MX host
    list from [./mx_routing.mdx](./mx_routing.mdx).
