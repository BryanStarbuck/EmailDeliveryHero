# EmailDeliveryHero — Sender Reputation Metrics Check

**Sender reputation** is the receiver-side scorecard that mailbox providers (Gmail, Yahoo,
Microsoft) keep on a sending **domain** and **IP**: how many recipients hit "Report spam", how much
mail hard-bounces, whether recipients open/reply (wanted) or delete-unread (unwanted), and how
steadily volume ramps. Unlike SPF/DKIM/DMARC — which are deterministic DNS facts this app can read
directly — reputation is **behavioral telemetry owned by the receivers and your ESP**. The 2024
[Gmail](https://support.google.com/mail/answer/81126) and
[Yahoo](https://senders.yahooinc.com/) bulk-sender requirements make it a hard gate: a bulk sender
(≥ 5,000 messages/day to Gmail) **must keep its Google-Postmaster-reported spam rate below 0.3% and
should stay under 0.1%**; sustained breaches throttle or reject the domain's mail regardless of
perfect authentication. This check surfaces those numbers, trends them over time, and tells the
operator exactly which lever to pull.

This check belongs to the **Spam & Content** dashboard category (reputation sub-family); all
sub-check ids use the **`content`** checkId prefix. It is implemented as
`reputation-metrics.checker.ts` (see [Engineering §6.3](../engineering.mdx)). Because its inputs are
third-party telemetry, **almost every sub-check is FUTURE-round, gated behind an integration** (Google
Postmaster Tools API, provider FBLs, ESP metrics) — the first round ships only the config/enrollment
advisories and the DNSBL cross-reference.

## 1. What it checks

Mailbox providers score every sender on signals recipients generate. The 2024 Gmail/Yahoo bulk-sender
rules codified previously-informal norms into pass/fail thresholds:

- **Spam-complaint rate** — the fraction of *delivered* mail that recipients mark as spam, as
  reported by **Google Postmaster Tools (GPT)**. Gmail's rule: **stay below 0.3%, target below
  0.1%**. Above 0.3% sustained, Gmail throttles and spam-folders the domain.
- **Domain & IP reputation** — GPT publishes a **High / Medium / Low / Bad** band per domain and per
  IP. "Low"/"Bad" means most mail lands in spam; recovery takes weeks of clean sending.
- **Hard-bounce rate** — invalid-recipient rejections (SMTP 5.1.1). High bounce rates signal a
  purchased/stale list and directly damage reputation; providers expect list hygiene.
- **Authentication pass rate** — GPT reports SPF/DKIM/DMARC pass percentages; the bulk rules require
  DMARC alignment. (This check *reports* GPT's view; the record correctness lives in
  [`spf`](./spf.mdx)/[`dkim`](./dkim.mdx)/`dmarc`.)
- **Engagement** — providers weight opens, replies, "not spam" recoveries, and folder placement.
  A sender whose mail is read and replied to looks *wanted*; one that is deleted-unread or
  spam-marked looks unwanted.
- **Warmup / volume consistency** — a brand-new IP or domain that suddenly blasts high volume ("cold
  start") is treated as a spammer. Reputation is earned by ramping volume gradually and sending
  **steady** daily volume.
- **Feedback loops (FBLs)** — provider programs (Yahoo CFL, Microsoft SNDS/JMRP, Comcast, etc.) that
  email the sender a copy of each complaint so the sender can suppress that recipient. If you are not
  **enrolled**, complaints happen invisibly and you cannot suppress the complainers.

**Why receivers care.** Authentication proves *who* sent the mail; reputation decides *whether they
want it*. A perfectly-authenticated domain with a 0.5% complaint rate will still be spam-foldered.

**Common failure modes.** (1) Complaint rate creeps over 0.3% after a bad campaign or a
re-permission blast → Gmail throttling. (2) New IP warmed too fast → "Bad" IP reputation for weeks.
(3) Never enrolled in FBLs → complaints invisible, same recipients complain repeatedly. (4) Hard-bounce
spike from an imported stale list → domain reputation drops. (5) Erratic volume (0 one day, 200k the
next) reads as a compromised/rented sender. (6) Recurring DNSBL listings that the operator keeps
"fixing" without addressing the root cause.

## 2. Sub-checks

Detection is mostly **ingestion**: the checker reads cached third-party metrics from the
`reputation_integrations`-backed connectors (GPT API, FBL mailbox parser, ESP metrics API) and
compares against thresholds. The **first round** ships only the sub-checks that need no external
data — enrollment/config advisories and the DNSBL history cross-reference.

| Sub-check (`checkId`) | What it verifies | How measured | Typical critical / warning finding | Concrete remediation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `content.complaint_rate` | Spam-complaint rate stays **< 0.3%** (Gmail hard limit) and ideally **< 0.1%**. | GPT `spamRate` (and FBL complaint count ÷ delivered) for the domain. | **critical:** ≥ 0.3% (Gmail throttle line); **warning:** 0.1%–0.3%. | Pause the offending campaign/segment; suppress recent complainers from FBL data; add [RFC 8058 one-click unsubscribe](./list_unsubscribe.mdx); re-permission or drop cold segments until rate < 0.1%. |
| `content.bounce_rate` | Hard-bounce rate is low (target **< 2%**, alarm ≥ 5%). | ESP hard-bounce count ÷ sent, per rolling window. | **critical:** ≥ 5% hard bounces; **warning:** 2%–5%. | Remove all hard-bounced addresses immediately; run list validation (verify MX/SMTP) before the next send; stop importing unverified lists. |
| `content.gpt_spam_rate` | GPT **User-reported spam rate** trend is flat/low, not climbing. | GPT `TrafficStats.spamRate` timeseries. | **critical:** spam rate ≥ 0.3% or a sharp upward slope; **warning:** 0.1%–0.3% or rising trend. | Identify the campaign/day the spike started (join to send logs); suppress that segment; slow volume until GPT rate recovers below 0.1%. |
| `content.gpt_domain_reputation` | GPT **domain reputation** band is **High** (or at least Medium). | GPT `TrafficStats.domainReputation` enum. | **critical:** `BAD` or `LOW`; **warning:** `MEDIUM`. | Cut volume to your most-engaged recipients only, fix complaint/bounce drivers, and hold steady 2–4 weeks; reputation is earned back slowly, not with a config change. |
| `content.gpt_ip_reputation` | GPT **IP reputation** band per sending IP is High/Medium. | GPT `TrafficStats.ipReputations[]` per IP. | **critical:** an IP is `BAD`/`LOW`; **warning:** `MEDIUM`. | For a shared-IP pool, ask the ESP to move you or investigate co-tenants; for a dedicated IP, re-warm gradually and reduce complaint sources. |
| `content.gpt_auth_rate` | GPT-reported SPF/DKIM/DMARC **pass percentages** are ~100% (aligned). | GPT `TrafficStats` `spfSuccessRatio` / `dkimSuccessRatio` / `dmarcSuccessRatio`. | **warning:** any ratio materially < 100% (some streams unauthenticated). | Trace the unaligned stream (forwarder, sub-mailer) and fix its SPF/DKIM alignment; cross-check [`./spf.mdx`](./spf.mdx), [`./dkim.mdx`](./dkim.mdx). |
| `content.fbl_enrollment` | Sending domain/IP is **enrolled in the relevant provider FBLs** so complaints are captured & suppressible. | Config presence in `reputation_integrations` + advisory list of programs for the sending IPs' networks. | **warning:** not enrolled in Yahoo CFL / Microsoft SNDS-JMRP / etc. | Enroll: Yahoo Complaint Feedback Loop, Microsoft SNDS + JMRP, Comcast/other FBLs; wire the FBL mailbox into your suppression pipeline. |
| `content.fbl_processing` | FBL complaints are actually **ingested and suppressed** (not just received). | FBL connector: complaints parsed in window vs suppressions applied. | **critical:** complaints received but recipients not suppressed (repeat complaints); **warning:** processing lag. | Automate: parse each FBL/ARF report, add the recipient to the global suppression list within 24h, never mail them again. |
| `content.engagement` | Positive-engagement signals (opens/clicks/replies, "not spam") dominate over deletes/spam-marks. | ESP engagement metrics + GPT complaint ratio proxy. | **warning:** low open/high delete-unread, or engagement declining over time. | Segment by engagement; suppress 90-day+ non-openers; send wanted content/cadence; sunset dormant subscribers. |
| `content.warmup` | New IP/domain **ramps volume gradually** — no cold-start spike. | Volume timeseries vs a warmup schedule (e.g. doubling daily cap) for domains flagged `is_new`/first-seen. | **critical:** brand-new IP/domain sent a large blast day one; **warning:** ramp faster than schedule. | Follow a warmup ramp (day 1: ~50/mailbox-provider, roughly double daily, prioritize most-engaged recipients) over 4–8 weeks; hold at each step if complaints rise. |
| `content.volume_consistency` | Day-to-day volume is **steady**, no wild spikes/troughs. | Coefficient of variation / day-over-day ratio on the send-volume series. | **warning:** volume swings > 5× day-over-day or long gaps then a blast. | Smooth sends across days; avoid "big Monday blast, silent all week"; spread large campaigns over a ramp. |
| `content.blocklist_history` *(advisory)* | Recurring DNSBL listings **over time** (a reputation pattern, not a point check). | Trend the per-run [`./blacklists.mdx`](./blacklists.mdx) results stored across audit runs. | **warning:** the domain/IP has been listed on the same DNSBL ≥ 2 times in the trailing window (recurring, not one-off). | Fix the *root cause* (complaint source / compromised account / open relay), not just the delisting request; cross-ref the current listing in [`./blacklists.mdx`](./blacklists.mdx). |
| `content.reputation_data_available` | At least one reputation source is **connected & fresh** so the other sub-checks are meaningful. | Check `reputation_integrations.enabled` + last-sync freshness. | **info:** no reputation integration connected → reputation sub-checks are "unknown"; **warning:** connected but data stale (> 3 days). | Connect Google Postmaster Tools (verify the domain in GPT, add the API credential) and/or your ESP metrics API in Settings → Integrations. |
| `content.postmaster_verified` | The domain is **verified in Google Postmaster Tools** (prerequisite for any GPT data). | GPT API returns data (not 403/empty) for the domain. | **warning:** domain not verified in GPT → no Gmail reputation visibility. | Add the domain in postmaster.google.com and publish the GPT TXT verification record (or reuse existing Search Console verification). |
| `content.delivery_errors` | GPT **delivery-error** categories (rate-limited, spam-rejected, policy) are low. | GPT `DeliveryErrors` breakdown. | **warning:** notable `RATE_LIMITED` / `SUSPECTED_SPAM` / `REJECTED_DUE_TO_...` share. | Address the specific error class Gmail reports (slow down for rate-limits; fix content/auth for spam rejects). |

> **First-round note:** only `content.fbl_enrollment`, `content.reputation_data_available`,
> `content.postmaster_verified` (config/advisory), and `content.blocklist_history` (trends data this
> app already stores from [`./blacklists.mdx`](./blacklists.mdx)) ship **first round**. Everything
> that consumes GPT, FBL, or ESP metrics is **future**, gated behind the corresponding integration.

## 3. How it is measured

Reputation is **not measured by DNS**; it is **ingested** from three connector types, each behind a
`reputation_integrations` config row, and compared to thresholds. The checker never blocks the audit
run: if a source is not connected, its sub-checks emit an `info` "unknown" finding rather than a false
negative.

**Connector A — Google Postmaster Tools (future).** The GPT API
(`gmailpostmastertools.googleapis.com/v1`) exposes per-domain `trafficStats` days:
`spamRate` (0–1 float), `domainReputation`/`ipReputations[].reputation`
(`HIGH|MEDIUM|LOW|BAD|REPUTATION_CATEGORY_UNSPECIFIED`), `spf/dkim/dmarcSuccessRatio`,
`deliveryErrors[]`, and `userReportedSpamRatio`. Auth is OAuth2 (service account or user consent)
stored via the credential store referenced in [Engineering](../engineering.mdx). The connector
**caches** the timeseries locally so a 6h audit does not re-pull the full history; GPT itself lags
~2–3 days and has daily granularity, so a nightly sync is sufficient and well within quota. Threshold
map: `spamRate ≥ 0.003` → `content.complaint_rate`/`content.gpt_spam_rate` **critical**;
`0.001–0.003` → **warning**. `domainReputation`/`ipReputation` `BAD|LOW` → **critical**, `MEDIUM` →
**warning**, `HIGH` → **ok**.

**Connector B — Feedback loops (future).** An FBL mailbox (or provider portal/API) receives ARF
(RFC 5965) complaint reports. The connector IMAP-polls the mailbox, parses ARF, counts complaints per
domain/IP per window, and cross-checks that each complained recipient was added to suppression.
`content.fbl_enrollment` is **config-only** (are we enrolled for the networks our `sendingIps`
belong to?) and therefore ships first round as an advisory; `content.fbl_processing` needs the live
mailbox and is future. Complaint rate from FBL is a fallback when GPT is absent
(`complaints ÷ delivered`).

**Connector C — ESP metrics (future).** Most ESPs (SendGrid, Mailgun, Postmark, SES, etc.) expose
sent/delivered/bounce/complaint/open/click counts via API. The connector normalizes these into the
same `reputation_metrics` shape, driving `content.bounce_rate`, `content.engagement`,
`content.warmup`, and `content.volume_consistency`. Warmup/volume use the **send-volume timeseries**:
`content.warmup` compares a domain first-seen within N days against an expected ramp (flag a large day-1
blast); `content.volume_consistency` computes day-over-day ratio / coefficient of variation and warns
on > 5× swings or long silence then a spike.

**Timeseries / trend nature.** Reputation is inherently **many rows over time**, unlike the
single-record DNS checks. The checker's job each run is to (a) pull/refresh the timeseries into
`reputation_metrics` (many `measured_at` rows), and (b) evaluate the *latest window* plus the *slope*
into findings. `content.blocklist_history` is the pure-trend example that needs **no** external feed:
it reads the app's own prior `blacklists` results across `audit_runs` and flags recurrence.

**Map to severities.** Latest-window value vs threshold sets severity; a worsening slope escalates a
warning toward critical (e.g. spam rate 0.12% but doubling week-over-week → warning with an upward-trend
note). Missing data → `info` "unknown", never critical. All non-ok findings quote the **observed
number** and the concrete operational lever (pause segment, suppress, warm slower, enroll FBL).

## 4. User interface

Reputation surfaces through the **Spam & Content dashboard column**, the **audit-detail view**, and a
dedicated **Reputation panel** with trend sparklines, on the React 19 + Vite + Tailwind + shadcn/ui +
lucide-react stack.

**Dashboard cell.** Reputation findings feed the shared **Spam & Content** cell (with
`content_scoring`, `list_unsubscribe`, etc.). Color = worst `content.*` severity for the latest audit.
A `BAD` GPT reputation or ≥ 0.3% complaint rate turns it **dark red**; `info` "unknown" (no integration)
never turns it amber — a small "not connected" dot indicates missing data instead.

**Audit-detail view.** Findings render as a shadcn `Accordion`/`Card` group titled **Sender
Reputation**, one row per non-ok finding, with an inline **sparkline** (last 30 days) since these are
timeseries:

```
┌─ Sender Reputation ─────────────────────── [ CRITICAL ] ─┐
│ content.complaint_rate ⛔ Spam complaints 0.41% (>0.30%) │
│   Observed : GPT spamRate 0.0041, ▁▂▃▅█ rising 7d         │
│   Fix      : Pause "reactivation" segment; suppress FBL   │
│             complainers; enforce one-click unsub  [ Copy ]│
├───────────────────────────────────────────── [ WARNING ]─┤
│ content.warmup        ⚠ New IP ramped too fast (day 2)   │
│   Fix      : Cap ~2x/day over 6 wks, engaged-first [ Copy ]│
├─ content.reputation_data_available  ℹ Not connected ─────┤
│   Fix      : Connect Google Postmaster Tools     [ Copy ] │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Each row: severity `Badge` (lucide `TrendingDown`/`ShieldAlert`/`Activity`), the `title`, the observed
`detail` (number + `Sparkline`), the `remediation`, and a **Copy fix** button. A dedicated
**Reputation panel** on the domain detail page charts `spamRate`, `bounce_rate`, `domainReputation`
band, and daily volume over time (shadcn `Chart`/`recharts`), with the 0.3%/0.1% threshold lines drawn.

**Per-domain config inputs.** Settings → **Integrations** holds the per-domain integration rows: a
**Google Postmaster Tools** connector (OAuth), an **ESP metrics** connector (provider + API key), and
an **FBL** connector (mailbox IMAP or portal). Each shows connected/last-synced status. **Empty/never-run:**
light-gray cell + "Connect a reputation source" CTA. **Loading:** skeleton sparkline. **All-ok:** a
single green "Reputation High — complaints 0.04%, bounces 0.6%" summary. **Admin-only settings**
(Settings → Admin): complaint-rate warning/critical thresholds (defaults 0.1% / 0.3%), bounce
thresholds (2% / 5%), warmup schedule, and integration credentials (admin-only).

## 5. Information schema (database schema)

Reputation extends the **shared core tables** (owned by engineering — do not redefine): `domains`,
`sending_ips`, `audit_runs`, and `findings`. Each sub-check emits a row into shared `findings`
(`category='content'`, `check_id='content.complaint_rate'`, etc.). Reputation adds a **timeseries
result table** (`reputation_metrics`, many rows over time — the crux of this check) and an
**integration config table** (`reputation_integrations`).

```sql
-- Timeseries of ingested reputation metrics. MANY rows over time (per day / per source),
-- not one row per audit run — this is the trend/timeseries table.
CREATE TABLE reputation_metrics (
  id             BIGINT GENERATED ALWAYS AS IDENTITY PRIMARY KEY,
  audit_run_id   BIGINT REFERENCES audit_runs(id) ON DELETE SET NULL,  -- run that ingested/observed it (nullable: nightly sync may precede an audit)
  domain_id      BIGINT NOT NULL REFERENCES domains(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
  sending_ip_id  BIGINT REFERENCES sending_ips(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,  -- NULL = domain-level metric
  complaint_rate NUMERIC(6,5),          -- 0.00000–1.00000 (e.g. 0.00300 = 0.3%)
  bounce_rate    NUMERIC(6,5),          -- hard-bounce fraction
  spam_rate      NUMERIC(6,5),          -- GPT user-reported spam rate
  gpt_domain_rep TEXT,                  -- 'HIGH' | 'MEDIUM' | 'LOW' | 'BAD' | NULL
  gpt_ip_rep     TEXT,                  -- per-IP band when sending_ip_id set
  spf_pass_rate  NUMERIC(6,5),          -- GPT auth success ratios
  dkim_pass_rate NUMERIC(6,5),
  dmarc_pass_rate NUMERIC(6,5),
  volume         BIGINT,                -- messages sent that day (for warmup/consistency)
  open_rate      NUMERIC(6,5),          -- engagement proxies (ESP)
  click_rate     NUMERIC(6,5),
  source         TEXT NOT NULL,         -- 'google_postmaster' | 'fbl' | 'esp'
  measured_at    TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL,  -- the day/window the metric applies to (provider's date)
  created_at     TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT now(),
  UNIQUE (domain_id, sending_ip_id, source, measured_at)  -- idempotent re-sync
);
CREATE INDEX idx_repmetrics_domain_time ON reputation_metrics(domain_id, measured_at DESC);
CREATE INDEX idx_repmetrics_run         ON reputation_metrics(audit_run_id);
CREATE INDEX idx_repmetrics_source      ON reputation_metrics(domain_id, source, measured_at DESC);

-- Per-domain reputation integration configuration + sync state.
CREATE TABLE reputation_integrations (
  id              BIGINT GENERATED ALWAYS AS IDENTITY PRIMARY KEY,
  domain_id       BIGINT NOT NULL REFERENCES domains(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
  provider        TEXT   NOT NULL,      -- 'google_postmaster' | 'fbl' | 'esp'
  credentials_ref TEXT,                 -- opaque handle into the secret store (never the secret itself)
  config          JSONB,               -- provider specifics (ESP name, FBL mailbox, GPT project)
  enabled         BOOLEAN NOT NULL DEFAULT FALSE,
  last_synced_at  TIMESTAMPTZ,          -- freshness for content.reputation_data_available
  last_sync_error TEXT,
  created_at      TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT now(),
  updated_at      TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT now(),
  UNIQUE (domain_id, provider)
);
CREATE INDEX idx_repintegrations_domain ON reputation_integrations(domain_id, enabled);
```

**Mapping to today's JSON file store.** There is no database yet (see
[Engineering §7](../engineering.mdx)). This check's **timeseries nature clashes with the per-run audit
file**, so it maps in two places:

- **Config** lives in the global `~/.email_delivery_hero/config.yaml` (or a sibling
  `integrations.yaml`) under `reputationIntegrations[]` — the future `reputation_integrations` table.
  Secrets are referenced by handle, stored in the OS keychain / secret store, not in the YAML.
- **Metrics** are **not** naturally one-per-audit. Today they are appended to a per-domain rolling file
  `~/.email_delivery_hero/reputation/<domainId>.jsonl` (one JSON line per `(source, measured_at)`),
  which *is* the `reputation_metrics` table row-for-row (the JSONL append maps to the
  `UNIQUE(domain_id, sending_ip_id, source, measured_at)` upsert). Each audit run then reads the
  latest window from that file and writes only the **derived findings** into
  `audits/<domainId>/<auditId>.json` under `results.reputation` (latest values + slope + the finding
  set):

```jsonc
// audits/<domainId>/<auditId>.json  →  results.reputation  (derived snapshot; full series is in reputation/<domainId>.jsonl)
{
  "window": "2026-06-24..2026-06-30",
  "complaintRate": 0.0041, "bounceRate": 0.006, "spamRate": 0.0041,
  "gptDomainRep": "LOW", "gptIpRep": { "203.0.113.10": "MEDIUM" },
  "trend": { "spamRate": "rising", "volumeCv": 0.9 },
  "sources": ["google_postmaster"], "dataFresh": true
}
```

The `store` module is the only code touching these shapes, so promoting the JSONL series into
`reputation_metrics` and the YAML config into `reputation_integrations` is a single-module change.

## 6. When we run the check

- **On-demand:** every manual audit (`POST /api/domains/:id/audit`) and "run now"
  (`POST /api/scheduler/run`) runs `reputation-metrics.checker.ts`. First round it only evaluates the
  config/advisory + `content.blocklist_history` sub-checks; the metric sub-checks emit `info`
  "not connected" until an integration is configured.
- **Periodic:** the in-process scheduler re-runs the engine per domain on the `config.yaml` cadence
  (default 6h). Reputation **ingestion**, however, is decoupled: a separate **nightly sync** job pulls
  GPT/FBL/ESP data into the timeseries (GPT lags 2–3 days and is daily-granular, so hourly polling is
  wasteful). The 6h audit reads whatever the last sync stored — it does not itself call third-party APIs.
- **First-round vs future:** `content.fbl_enrollment`, `content.reputation_data_available`,
  `content.postmaster_verified`, and `content.blocklist_history` are **first round**. All GPT/FBL/ESP
  metric sub-checks are **future**, each gated behind its `reputation_integrations` row being
  `enabled`.
- **Cost / rate-limits / back-off:** GPT has generous quotas but should be pulled once/day per domain;
  the connector caches and only fetches new `measured_at` days (incremental). ESP/FBL polling respects
  provider limits with exponential back-off; a failed sync sets `last_sync_error` and the audit reports
  stale-data (`content.reputation_data_available` warning) rather than failing.
- **Regression detection:** each run's reputation findings are diffed against the previous run
  (Engineering §8). A newly-appearing or worsened finding — complaint rate crossing 0.1% then 0.3%, a
  GPT band dropping High→Medium→Low, a bounce spike, a warmup violation, or a recurring DNSBL listing —
  is flagged as a **new problem** on the Dashboard. Because these are trends, the diff also captures
  *slope* changes, not just threshold crossings.

## 8. Acceptance criteria

1. A domain with **no reputation integration connected** yields `content.reputation_data_available`
   **info** ("unknown"), and every metric sub-check emits `info` "not connected" — never a false
   critical, and the Spam & Content cell is not turned amber by these `info` findings.
2. With **Google Postmaster Tools** connected, a domain whose GPT `spamRate` ≥ **0.003** yields
   `content.complaint_rate` / `content.gpt_spam_rate` **critical** quoting the observed percentage;
   **0.001–0.003** yields **warning**; < 0.001 yields **ok**.
3. GPT `domainReputation` of `BAD`/`LOW` yields `content.gpt_domain_reputation` **critical**, `MEDIUM`
   **warning**, `HIGH` **ok**; per-IP bands drive `content.gpt_ip_reputation` identically.
4. With ESP metrics connected, a hard-bounce rate ≥ **5%** yields `content.bounce_rate` **critical**,
   2–5% **warning**, and the remediation names list validation + immediate hard-bounce suppression.
5. A brand-new IP/domain (first-seen within the warmup window) that sent a large day-one blast yields
   `content.warmup` **critical** with a concrete ramp schedule; day-over-day volume swings > 5× yield
   `content.volume_consistency` **warning**.
6. `content.fbl_enrollment` warns when the domain/IP is not enrolled in the relevant provider FBLs and
   names the exact programs to join (Yahoo CFL, Microsoft SNDS/JMRP, etc.); `content.fbl_processing`
   flags complaints received but recipients not suppressed.
7. `content.blocklist_history` warns when the same DNSBL has listed the domain/IP **≥ 2 times** in the
   trailing window, cross-referencing the current state in [`./blacklists.mdx`](./blacklists.mdx), and
   requires **no** external integration.
8. Every non-`ok` reputation finding carries a **concrete operational** remediation (pause segment,
   suppress complainers, warm slower, enroll FBL, connect GPT) — never a generic "improve reputation".
9. Ingested metrics are persisted as a **timeseries** (`reputation_metrics` / `reputation/<domainId>.jsonl`)
   with a unique `(domain_id, sending_ip_id, source, measured_at)` key so re-syncs are idempotent, and
   the derived snapshot maps cleanly onto `results.reputation` in the audit JSON.
10. Integration config (provider, enabled, credentials_ref, last_synced_at) is persisted in
    `reputation_integrations` / `config.yaml`, with secrets held by reference only (never in plaintext
    YAML).
11. The nightly sync pulls only new `measured_at` days (incremental), applies exponential back-off on
    provider errors, and on failure sets `content.reputation_data_available` to a stale-data **warning**
    rather than failing the audit.
12. The scheduler re-runs reputation each cadence and flags any newly-appearing or worsened finding —
    including **trend/slope** changes, not just threshold crossings — as a new problem.
